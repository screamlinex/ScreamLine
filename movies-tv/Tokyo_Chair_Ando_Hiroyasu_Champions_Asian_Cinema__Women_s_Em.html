Tokyo Chair Ando Hiroyasu Champions Asian Cinema, Women’s Empowerment at 2025 Festival

Summary: As the Tokyo International Film Festival launches its 2025 edition, Chair Ando Hiroyasu is steering the event toward a future that prioritizes Asian cinema discovery, gender equality, and cross‑cultural dialogue. Drawing on decades of diplomatic experience, Ando aims to transform TIFF from a local showcase into a global platform that amplifies unheard voices and fosters international collaboration.

Chairman’s Vision for the Future

Ando Hiroyasu articulated a bold roadmap at the opening ceremony: “My goal is to make TIFF a bridge between Asia’s diverse film communities and the rest of the world.” He plans to expand the festival’s programming to feature more first‑time filmmakers from emerging markets, while introducing a mentorship initiative that pairs seasoned directors with rising talent.

Asian Cinema Spotlight

The 2025 lineup showcases an unprecedented 40% increase in Asian titles, including a special retrospective of Korean New Wave classics and a curated segment on Southeast Asian independent cinema. Ando emphasizes that “discovering new stories is essential to preserving cultural heritage and inspiring future generations.”

Women Empowerment Initiatives

In line with his commitment to gender equality, the festival has launched a “Women of Film” forum featuring panel discussions, workshops, and a dedicated award for best female‑directed feature. The initiative aims to boost the visibility of women filmmakers and create networking opportunities that translate into industry‑wide change.

Cross‑Cultural Dialogue Through Film

Leveraging his diplomatic background, Ando has introduced a “Dialogue Dinners” series where filmmakers, critics, and cultural leaders meet to discuss the social impact of cinema. These gatherings are designed to spark conversations that go beyond the screen, encouraging collaboration on projects that address shared regional challenges.
