A Contracorriente Picks Up for Spain Cohn and Duprat’s ‘Homo Sapiens?’ Sold by Latido, Setting a Dec. 25 Theatrical Release (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: A Contracorriente Films has secured the Spanish theatrical rights to Gastón Duprat and Mariano Cohn’s black‑comedy anthology “Homo Sapiens?” (original title “Homo Argentum”), with a debut at the end of 2023 and a December 25 release in Spain. The acquisition, handled through distributor Latido Films, follows sales to Israel, Greece, Cyprus and the English‑speaking market. The film, which debuted in the official competition at the 2023 Cannes Film Festival, promises a sharp, satirical look at contemporary Argentine society.

Festival Roots and Critical Buzz

“Homo Argentum” premiered in the Official Competition at Cannes, where it garnered praise for its dark humor and bold narrative structure. Critics highlighted Duprat and Cohn’s signature blend of absurdity and social commentary, noting the film’s ability to balance satire with genuine pathos.

Distribution Strategy

Latido Films, the Spanish arm of A Contracorriente, will coordinate a December 25 theatrical rollout across major Spanish cities, positioning the film as a holiday audience draw. The partnership also includes targeted marketing in key markets such as Israel, Greece, and English‑speaking Cyprus, ensuring a wide international reach.

Cultural Impact

By bringing this anthology to Spanish screens, A Contracorriente is tapping into the growing appetite for Latin American cinema that tackles contemporary issues with humor. “Homo Argentum” offers a mirror to Spanish audiences, reflecting shared cultural anxieties about identity, politics, and the human condition.

What to Expect

Audiences can anticipate a series of vignettes that weave together disparate stories—ranging from a disillusioned farmer to a cynical urban entrepreneur—each delivering biting social critique while maintaining a relentless comedic rhythm. The film’s unique structure and sharp dialogue make it a standout addition to the 2023‑24 film lineup.
