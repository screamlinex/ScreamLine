Ayushmann Khurrana Celebrates Box Office Triumph of Maddock Horror Comedy Universe Film ‘Thamma’: ‘All My Dreams Come True as an Entertainer’ (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: Bollywood star Ayushmann Khurrana has celebrated a milestone by bagging his fifth 100‑crore hit with the Diwali release “Thamma.” The supernatural horror‑comedy, directed by Aditya Sarpotdar and produced by Dinesh Vijan, has surged past the ₹100 crore mark and is on track to become the highest‑grossing film in the Maddock Horror Comedy Universe. Khurrana, who has said the film’s success feels like “all my dreams come true as an entertainer,” credits the blend of humour, chills and a star‑packed cast for its box‑office triumph.

Box Office Milestone Reached

“Thamma” crossed the ₹100 crore threshold on its fifth day of release, marking a significant achievement for Ayushmann Khurrana. With a global earnings of roughly $11.3 million, the film now stands as the sixth highest‑grossing Indian film of the year and a front‑runner in the Maddock Horror Comedy Universe. The success is a testament to the film’s strong word‑of‑mouth, strategic release timing around the Diwali festival, and robust digital marketing campaign.

Ayushmann’s Take on the Triumph

Speaking after the film’s release, Ayushmann said, “I’ve always believed in creating content that makes people laugh and think at the same time. Seeing ‘Thamma’ hit the 100‑crore mark feels like all my dreams come true as an entertainer. I’m grateful to the crew, the cast, and the audience for this incredible support.” He added that the success would fuel future projects that push creative boundaries.

Creative Blend of Horror and Comedy

Director Aditya Sarpotdar crafted a narrative that balances eerie suspense with slapstick moments. The screenplay, written by Sarpotdar and Pankaj Kapoor, weaves supernatural folklore with contemporary humour, making it accessible to a wide demographic. Critics praised the film’s pacing, visual effects, and the way it subverts classic horror tropes.

Key Cast and Crew Contributions

Alongside Ayushmann, the film features Ananya Panday, Rajesh Sharma, and veteran comedian Anupam Kher, whose performances added depth to the comedic arcs. Cinematographer Karthik Venkatesh captured the film’s atmospheric settings, while music director Shantanu Moitra supplied an eclectic score that blends traditional Indian instruments with modern beats. The combined talent of the cast and crew has been pivotal to the movie’s appeal.

Future of the Maddock Horror Comedy Universe

With “Thamma” now setting new benchmarks, Dinesh Vijan’s production house is exploring spin‑offs and sequels that further expand the universe. Rumoured titles include “Ghoul’s Reign” and “Spooky Sisters,” both slated for release in the next two years. The franchise aims to maintain its unique mix of terror and laughter while exploring fresh storylines and characters.
