‘Host’ Star Haley Bishop to Lead Action-Horror ‘For the Good of the Herd’ as Clever Creature Boards Ahead of AFM (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: Toronto’s Clever Creature Inc. will bring the action‑horror feature “For the Good of the Herd” to the American Film Market, with “Host” alum Haley Bishop attached to star. Written and directed by Mark Pritchard, the film is slated to start principal photography in the UK in November.

Premise

In a world where a ruthless corporation has engineered a herd‑like society of obedient workers, a lone survivor—played by Bishop—uncovers a sinister plot that threatens to turn the entire herd into a living weapon. With high‑octane chase sequences and bone‑cracking stunts, the film blends survival horror with a critique of corporate control.

Cast

Haley Bishop takes on the lead role, bringing the same razor‑sharp intensity she showcased in “Host.” Supporting the cast are rising stars and seasoned character actors, including an unannounced veteran known for his work in indie thrillers, adding depth to the film’s moral dilemmas.

Production

Mark Pritchard, the writer‑director behind the critically acclaimed short “The Orchard,” has been tasked with translating his dark, atmospheric vision to a feature‑length format. Production will shoot on location across the UK’s moorlands and industrial sites, providing the perfect backdrop for the film’s bleak, dystopian aesthetic.

Release & Market

Clever Creature will debut the film at the American Film Market, aiming to secure a distribution partner for a summer 2025 release. The market premiere positions the project to attract both genre‑specific buyers and mainstream distributors looking for fresh horror‑action hybrids.

Creative Vision

“For the Good of the Herd” is described by Pritchard as “a visceral exploration of conformity and resistance.” He cites the influence of classic exploitation cinema, while integrating modern CGI to create the film’s signature herd‑like creatures that move as a single entity, amplifying the terror on screen.
