Paul Schrader on ‘Mishima’ Finally Screening at Tokyo Film Festival and Why He Thinks ‘People Will Pay’ to Watch AI Actors: ‘Use a Bit of Brando and Kevin Costner and Make an Exciting Movie’

Summary: Paul Schrader graced the 38th Tokyo International Film Festival’s opening night with enthusiasm, celebrating the 40th anniversary of his biopic “Mishima: A Life In Four Chapters.” The director reflected on his deep affection for Japan, called it his “second home,” and shared his bold vision that audiences will willingly pay to see movies starring AI actors—drawing parallels with the iconic screen presence of Brando and Kevin Costner.

Red‑Carpet Warm‑Up and Tokyo as Schrader’s “Second Home”

When Paul Schrader stepped onto the crimson carpet, the buzz was electric. The veteran filmmaker, who grew up in the U.S. but has lived in Japan for decades, described Tokyo as his “second home” and praised the festival’s commitment to celebrating global cinema. In a brief interview, he praised the city’s vibrant film culture and said that “the energy here feels like a second coming of my first love, Hollywood.”

Revisiting Mishima: 40 Years of a Biopic

“Mishima” premiered in 1985 to critical acclaim, charting the rise and tragic fall of Japanese playwright Yukio Mishima. Now, the film is screened in its restored form, complete with original score and newly recovered footage. Schrader noted that the story’s themes of identity, ambition, and the clash between tradition and modernity remain resonant for today’s audiences.

AI Actors and the Future of Pay‑Per‑View Cinema

In a striking revelation, Schrader said he believes audiences will pay to watch AI‑generated actors in the same way they pay for blockbuster stars. He argued that “the allure of cinema lies in the emotional connection to a character,” and that a well‑crafted AI can deliver that. He cited the screen presence of Brando and Kevin Costner as benchmarks: “If an AI can channel the gravitas of Brando or the charisma of Costner, people will line up for the experience.”

Audience Reaction and the Road Ahead

Festival-goers responded with curiosity and excitement, many taking selfies with holographic projections of Mishima’s famous monologues. Schrader’s comments sparked lively discussions among critics about the ethical and artistic implications of synthetic performers. He concluded that “cinema is always evolving, and if we can harness AI responsibly, we’ll open a new chapter in storytelling.”
