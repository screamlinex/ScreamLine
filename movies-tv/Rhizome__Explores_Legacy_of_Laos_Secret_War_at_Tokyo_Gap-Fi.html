‘Rhizome’ Explores Legacy of Laos Secret War at Tokyo Gap-Financing Market

Summary: “Rhizome,” a feature film that probes the buried trauma of America’s “Secret War” in Laos, is now seeking co‑producers and sales agents at the Tokyo Gap‑Financing Market. Directed by Rotterdam‑award winner Jakrawal Nilthamrong and produced by Chatchai Chaiyon, the film follows Na, a Lao‑American caught between the past and present, as he confronts the ghosts of war that still haunt the Mekong Valley. Production is slated for 2026, and the project looks to secure the financial and distribution partners needed to bring this powerful story to an international audience.

Film Synopsis

“Rhizome” follows Na (played by rising Lao‑American star Ananta Phomphak), a young archaeologist who discovers a forgotten stash of unexploded ordnance in the hills of Luang Prabang. As she digs deeper, she unearths letters, photographs, and a diary that reveal the clandestine U.S. bombing campaign that devastated Laos during the 1960s and 1970s. The film weaves Na’s personal journey with flashbacks to the war’s atrocities, exploring themes of memory, identity, and the lingering impact of foreign intervention on local communities.

Director & Producer

Jakrawal Nilthamrong, the Rotterdam winner of “Vanishing Point” and Venice selection “Anatomy of Time,” brings a distinct visual style that blends poetic realism with gritty documentary‑inspired footage. Nilthamrong’s previous work has earned acclaim for its lyrical treatment of historical trauma and cultural displacement.

Producer Chatchai Chaiyon, known for “Manta Ray,” is committed to elevating Southeast Asian stories on the global stage. His track record of securing international co‑production deals and navigating complex funding landscapes positions “Rhizome” for a successful launch.

Tokyo Gap‑Financing Market

At this year’s Tokyo Gap‑Financing Market, the team will present a 20‑minute teaser, a detailed business plan, and a robust distribution strategy targeting markets in Asia, Europe, and North America. The goal is to secure both production financing and a sales agent who can navigate the competitive festival circuit and secure theatrical releases.

Production & Funding

Filming will begin in 2026, with principal locations in Laos, Thailand, and the Netherlands. The project is looking for a 40‑percent gap financing partner, along with pre‑sales agreements that cover key territories. Nilthamrong and Chaiyon are open to joint‑venture agreements, equity participation, and in‑kind contributions such as post‑production facilities and festival promotion.
