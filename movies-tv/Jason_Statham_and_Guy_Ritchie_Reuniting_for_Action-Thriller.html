Jason Statham and Guy Ritchie Reuniting for Action-Thriller ‘Viva La Madness,’ Black Bear Launching at AFM (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: Guy Ritchie and Jason Statham’s long‑standing partnership is set to extend to a sixth feature, as the duo reunite for the action‑thriller “Viva La Madness,” Variety confirms. The film, based on J.J. Connolly’s novel and its sequel to “Layer Cake,” remains shrouded in secrecy, but insiders say it promises the same razor‑sharp wit and kinetic energy that defined their past collaborations.

Statham & Ritchie Reuniting After a Decade

After their explosive first collaboration in “Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels” and the critically acclaimed “Snatch,” Statham and Ritchie reunited for “The Gentlemen” and “Wrath of Man.” The latest partnership marks a return to the stylistic blend of fast cuts, snappy dialogue, and high‑octane set pieces that fans have come to love.

Sources close to the production say the director is eager to revisit the dynamic that made their earlier films cult classics, while Statham is keen to explore a new character that pushes his physical and comedic range to new limits.

Plot & Source Material: A Layer Cake Legacy

“Viva La Madness” adapts J.J. Connolly’s novel, a sequel to the bestselling “Layer Cake.” Although the script remains under wraps, early reports suggest the story follows a charismatic gangster who navigates a treacherous underworld while grappling with shifting allegiances.

Connolly’s trademark blend of gritty realism and dark humor is expected to translate into a screenplay that balances intense action sequences with razor‑sharp dialogue, a hallmark of Ritchie’s direction.

Production & Technical Vision

Filming kicked off in late 2024 in London’s industrial districts, with Ritchie employing his signature kinetic camera work and rapid editing style. The production team has highlighted the use of practical stunts over CGI to preserve the visceral feel of the franchise.

Statham’s stunt coordinator, who has worked with the actor on multiple projects, confirmed that the film will feature a new set of high‑risk action set pieces that showcase Statham’s signature mix of martial arts and gunplay.

Release Strategy & Marketing Buzz

“Viva La Madness” is slated for a limited theatrical release in the UK, with a broader global rollout planned for early 2026. The marketing campaign is already generating buzz, with teaser footage hinting at explosive set pieces and a soundtrack curated by Ritchie’s long‑time collaborator.

Industry analysts predict strong box‑office returns, citing the proven chemistry between Statham and Ritchie, the cult following of Connolly’s novels, and the current appetite for high‑energy action thrillers.
