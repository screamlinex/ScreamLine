Snuff Film Thriller ‘Hollywood Hells’ Boarded by Black Mandala Ahead of AFM (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: New Zealand-based genre sales outfit Black Mandala has boarded “Hollywood Hells,” a thriller that takes aim at the entertainment industry’s dark side, ahead of the upcoming American Film Market (AFM). “When a struggling actress attends an exclusive Hollywood party, she discovers a cabal of power brokers who produce real‑life snuff films for the elite.”

Film Overview

“Hollywood Hells” follows Maya Quinn, a fledgling actress desperate for a breakthrough. At a glittering gala hosted by the city’s most influential moguls, she stumbles upon a clandestine operation that turns the glitz of Tinseltown into a nightmare. As Maya digs deeper, she uncovers a network of executives who finance and star in real‑life snuff films for a wealthy, secretive clientele. The film blends psychological horror with a biting satire of Hollywood’s excesses, promising to keep audiences on the edge of their seats.

Black Mandala’s Involvement

Black Mandala’s acquisition of the project signals the firm’s growing appetite for edgy, high‑concept thrillers. The company will spearhead international sales, leveraging its strong foothold in Asia and the Middle East to secure lucrative distribution deals. Black Mandala’s track record—highlighted by recent successes like “Blood Moon” and “Midnight Runners”—positions it to navigate the complex rights landscape surrounding a film that tackles such a controversial subject matter.

AFM Premiere & Distribution Plans

Set to debut at AFM next week, “Hollywood Hells” will be showcased in the festival’s high‑profile “Genre Spotlight” section. Black Mandala’s strategy includes a simultaneous theatrical release in key markets followed by a strategic VOD launch on premium streaming platforms. The team is also exploring a limited theatrical run in select European cities to build buzz before a global rollout.

Industry Buzz & Critical Expectations

Early reactions from buyers and critics suggest “Hollywood Hells” could become a talking point for its audacious premise and razor‑sharp commentary on Hollywood’s underbelly. Film insiders anticipate that the movie’s blend of thriller pacing and social critique will resonate with audiences craving fresh, boundary‑pushing narratives. With Black Mandala’s backing, the film is poised to make a splash both at AFM and across the international marketplace.
