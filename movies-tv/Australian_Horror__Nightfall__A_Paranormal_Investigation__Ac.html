Australian Horror ‘Nightfall: A Paranormal Investigation’ Acquired by Black Mandala (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: New Zealand‑based sales powerhouse Black Mandala has secured distribution rights for the Australian horror feature “Nightfall: A Paranormal Investigation,” a 1988‑set paranormal thriller helmed by directors Myles McEwen and Ripley Stevens. Announced during TIFFCOM, the Tokyo International Film Festival’s market arm, the deal underscores the company’s ambition to build a franchise around the film’s chilling concept.

Deal Details and Market Context

Black Mandala’s acquisition reflects the growing appetite for Australian genre cinema in global markets. The company, known for its strategic sales of high‑concept titles, will handle worldwide distribution with a focus on streaming, theatrical, and festival circuits. The Tokyo market provided a prime platform for the deal, capitalizing on the festival’s reputation for spotlighting fresh horror voices.

Film Synopsis and Creative Team

Set in 1988, “Nightfall” follows seasoned investigator Mick Sutherland (played by local veteran actor) as he delves into a series of unexplained phenomena in a small coastal town. The film blends atmospheric dread with supernatural intrigue, drawing inspiration from classic 80s horror while employing modern visual storytelling techniques. Co‑directors Myles McEwen and Ripley Stevens bring a blend of atmospheric tension and character‑driven drama, supported by a screenplay that balances eerie pacing with sharp dialogue.

Franchise Potential and Strategic Vision

Black Mandala’s spokesperson highlighted the film’s “rich mythos” as a foundation for a multi‑entry franchise. The narrative’s open‑ended structure allows for sequels that could explore deeper lore or spin‑off stories within the same universe. The sales outfit plans to position “Nightfall” as a flagship title for a potential anthology series, leveraging its strong visual style and cult‑potential atmosphere.

Industry Reactions and Future Outlook

Festival buyers praised the film’s “distinctive blend of nostalgic horror and contemporary production values.” Analysts note that the timing of the acquisition—just as streaming services ramp up original horror content—positions Black Mandala to capitalize on both theatrical nostalgia and binge‑watch demand. The company intends to launch a multi‑platform campaign, targeting horror‑centric audiences worldwide.

Key Cast and Crew

In addition to lead actor Mick Sutherland, the film features a supporting cast of rising Australian talent, including newcomer Claire McKay in a pivotal role. Production was overseen by executive producer Emma Li, who previously worked on the critically acclaimed horror feature “Eclipse.” With a dedicated sound design team and a haunting score by composer Daniel Ruiz, “Nightfall” promises a sensory experience that will linger long after the credits roll.
