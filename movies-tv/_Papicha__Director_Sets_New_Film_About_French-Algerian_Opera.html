‘Papicha’ Director Sets New Film About French-Algerian Opera Singer Malika Bellaribi, Who Rose From the Parisian Slums (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: Mounia Meddour, whose feature debut “Papicha” opened at Cannes and won two César Awards in 2020, is now assembling an eclectic female ensemble for her next film, “Malika.” The biopic chronicles the true story of Malika Bellaribi, a French‑Algerian opera singer who rose from the Parisian projects, survived trauma, and ultimately became an international icon. Meddour’s new project promises a powerful exploration of identity, resilience, and the transformative power of music.

Director Mounia Meddour

After the critical acclaim of “Papicha,” Meddour is back with a more intimate, music‑driven narrative that reflects her own multicultural background. Known for her sharp eye for character and social commentary, she says the Malika story “touches every part of my heart” and will challenge audiences to rethink the intersection of art and adversity.

The Story of Malika Bellaribi

Born in the 13th arrondissement of Paris, Malika Bellaribi’s journey from the slums to the stages of the Paris Opera is a testament to perseverance. The film will cover her early life, the hardships she faced—including a traumatic assault—her discovery of opera, and her rise to international stardom, all while maintaining her Algerian roots.

Casting Highlights

The ensemble boasts a lineup of celebrated actresses and singers: Aïssa Maïga as Malika’s mother, Faye Dunaway in a cameo role, and Leïla Bekhti as a mentor figure. The production also features real opera singers in key roles, adding authenticity to the musical sequences.

Production Details

Filming began in late 2024 in the heart of Paris and the outskirts of Algiers. Meddour is collaborating with composer Houssem Khemiri to create a soundtrack that blends traditional Algerian melodies with classical opera. The film is slated for a limited theatrical release in France before a global streaming launch in early 2026.

Release & Impact

“Malika” is expected to premiere at the Cannes Film Festival’s “Un Certain Regard” section, where Meddour hopes to spark conversations about representation in cinema. Critics anticipate the film will resonate with audiences worldwide, offering a fresh perspective on the power of storytelling and the arts to transcend borders.
