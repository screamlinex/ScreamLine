Paramount Layoffs Underway: CEO David Ellison Says Cuts Are ‘Necessary’ for Company’s Long-Term Success

Summary: Paramount Skydance is laying off about 1,000 employees, mostly in the U.S. David Ellison, chairman and CEO of Paramount, sent employees a memo about the company‑wide cuts, saying “these steps are necessary to position Paramount for long‑term success.”

CEO’s Memo: A Call for Long‑Term Viability

In a blunt, company‑wide email, Ellison explained that the cuts are intended to streamline operations and reduce overhead as the studio pivots toward higher‑margin projects. “We want to be as open and direct as possible,” he wrote, acknowledging the emotional toll on staff while stressing that the restructuring is essential to keep Paramount competitive in a rapidly shifting media landscape.

Impact on Employees and Departments

Nearly 1,000 employees will be affected, with the bulk of layoffs occurring in the U.S. production, post‑production, and corporate support functions. The company has pledged to provide severance packages, outplacement services, and counseling for those impacted. While the cuts are broad, Ellison indicated that the studio will retain key talent in content development and streaming strategy to drive future growth.

Industry Context: A Broader Trend in Hollywood

Paramount’s decision follows a wave of layoffs across major studios and streaming platforms as the industry recalibrates after the COVID‑19 boom. Similar moves have been seen at Warner Bros., Disney, and Apple TV+, each aiming to tighten budgets and focus on high‑yield content. Analysts suggest that this shift reflects a growing emphasis on data‑driven content decisions and a move away from large‑budget, risk‑heavy productions.

Future Outlook and Strategic Priorities

Ellison highlighted the studio’s renewed focus on streaming, original content, and strategic partnerships. Paramount plans to accelerate its Paramount+ subscription growth, invest in genre‑specific programming, and explore new revenue streams such as short‑form content and esports. While the layoffs are a painful short‑term cost, the CEO argues they position the company for sustained profitability and a stronger competitive stance in the global media arena.
