‘Morte Cucina’ Team Says Asian Co-Productions Face Cash Flow Crisis – Tokyo Market

Summary: Film co‑production in Asia remains a labyrinth of logistics and finance, with cash‑flow issues at its core. The creative team behind the Tokyo International Film Festival competition entry “Morte Cucina”—a five‑nation effort spanning Thailand, Singapore, Taiwan, France, and Luxembourg—warned that juggling production budgets, currency swings, and investor timelines can stall projects in the market arm’s TIFFCOM seminar.

Cash Flow Crunch: A Real‑World Hurdle

Even after securing pre‑sales and equity, producers often find themselves short of working capital between shooting and distribution. “We’re paid in phases that don’t line up with the day‑to‑day expenses,” explained the film’s producer. The result? Delayed post‑production, strained relationships with crew, and, in worst cases, halted projects.

Currency volatility in Southeast Asia can amplify the problem, with budgets initially quoted in dollars suddenly requiring local currency that is weaker by the time funds are needed. “We’re forced to lock rates early, but the market moves fast,” added a co‑producer from Singapore.

Cross‑Border Coordination: More Than Just Language

Co‑productions span multiple legal frameworks, tax regimes, and creative visions. The “Morte Cucina” team highlighted how aligning schedules across five countries demanded a sophisticated project manager who could navigate each jurisdiction’s reporting requirements.
