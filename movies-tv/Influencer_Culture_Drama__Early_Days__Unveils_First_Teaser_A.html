Influencer Culture Drama ‘Early Days’ Unveils First Teaser Ahead of Red Sea Premiere (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: The first teaser for “Early Days,” a Mumbai‑set drama that exposes the dark side of influencer culture, has been released ahead of its world premiere at the Red Sea International Film Festival. The India‑Singapore co‑production, directed by feature‑debutant Priyankar Patra, is competing in the festival’s New Visions section (Dec. 4‑13) in Jeddah.

Red Sea Premiere

The film will make its international debut on the 4th of December at the Red Sea International Film Festival in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia. It will be showcased as part of the New Visions Competition, a segment dedicated to fresh storytelling and emerging filmmakers.

Storyline & Themes

“Early Days” follows the journey of a young social‑media star who rises to fame through a viral video series, only to find that the adoration comes at a steep cost. Set against the bustling backdrop of Mumbai, the narrative dives into the pressures of curated authenticity, the exploitation of audiences, and the psychological toll that a relentless digital spotlight can inflict.

Director & Creative Team

Priyankar Patra, who has previously helmed short‑film projects that garnered critical acclaim in India, steps into feature‑filmmaking with this debut. Co‑produced by partners in India and Singapore, the film boasts a strong ensemble cast and a cinematographer who captures Mumbai’s vibrant street life with raw intimacy.

Festival Significance

Being selected for the New Visions Competition places “Early Days” among a curated lineup of innovative voices from around the globe. The festival’s platform aims to spotlight narratives that challenge conventional perspectives—making this debut a timely conversation starter on the intersection of media, fame, and identity.
