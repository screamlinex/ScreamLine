LISTEN: How Rachel Sennott Planted Roots With ‘I Love L.A.’; Sarah Michelle Gellar Dishes on ‘Buffy’ Reboot; What Superfans See in ‘Love is Blind’

Summary: On today’s episode, Variety reporter Selome Hailu details her cover story on Rachel Sennott, the multihyphenate behind HBO’s new comedy “I Love L.A.” Marc Malkin has a Red Carpet Rundown with Sarah Michelle Gellar dishing about Hulu’s “Buffy the Vampire Slayer” reboot. And reporter Giana Levy explains what superfans see Netflix’s “Love Is Blind.” Hailu explores how Sennott’s personal roots in Southern California shape the show’s humor, while Malkin highlights Gellar’s excitement for revisiting a beloved icon and the creative challenges of a reboot. Rachel Sennott & the Roots of “I Love L.A.”

Selome Hailu’s feature on Rachel Sennott unpacks the comedian’s journey from stand‑up stages to the writers’ room of HBO’s new comedy “I Love L.A.” Sennott, who first gained national attention on “The Tonight Show” and “The Late Late Show,” brings a fresh, unapologetic perspective that blends her Southern California upbringing with her sharp observational humor. Hailu notes that Sennott’s “I Love L.A.” is less about the city’s glitz and more about the everyday absurdities that residents navigate—late‑night traffic, avocado toast, and the ever‑present reality of being a creative in a city that feels both welcoming and cutthroat. The article highlights how Sennott’s background as a writer, performer, and producer informs the show’s structure, ensuring each episode feels like a slice of life that is simultaneously satirical and deeply personal.

In addition to creative insights, Hailu discusses Sennott’s collaborative approach to building the cast and crew, emphasizing her commitment to fostering a supportive environment that encourages authenticity. The piece concludes with Sennott’s reflections on the future of comedy in a post‑pandemic landscape, where authenticity and relatability have become paramount.

Sarah Michelle Gellar on the Buffy Reboot

Marc Malkin’s Red Carpet Rundown captures Sarah Michelle Gellar’s candid enthusiasm for Hulu’s “Buffy the Vampire Slayer” reboot. Gellar, who famously portrayed the titular heroine in the 1990s, expresses both reverence for the original series and excitement about the fresh direction the reboot promises. She discusses how the new show tackles contemporary social issues—mental health, identity, and empowerment—while staying true to the core themes of friendship and resilience that made the original series iconic.

Gellar also talks about her own role in the project, shedding light on the challenges of reimagining a beloved character for a new generation. She emphasizes the importance of collaboration between the original creator, Joss Whedon’s creative team, and the new writers, ensuring the spirit of Buffy remains intact. The interview touches on Gellar’s personal growth as an actress and how this project aligns with her long‑term career goals, particularly her desire to be involved in projects that empower women and young people.

Superfans & the Allure of “Love Is Blind”

Reporter Giana Levy explores the phenomenon of superfans in Netflix’s “Love Is Blind,” revealing why the series continues to resonate beyond its initial season. Levy delves into the psychological dynamics that drive fan engagement, from the allure of the “blind” format—where participants meet and fall in love without visual cues—to the community-building aspects of online forums and fan conventions.
