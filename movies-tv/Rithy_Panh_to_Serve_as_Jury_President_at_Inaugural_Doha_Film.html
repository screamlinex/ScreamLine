Rithy Panh to Serve as Jury President at Inaugural Doha Film Festival

Summary: Cambodian auteur Rithy Panh has been appointed the jury president for the first edition of the Doha Film Festival, which will run from November 20–28 in Qatar’s capital. Panh, celebrated for his 2013 Cannes‑winning “The Missing Picture” and a recent Berlin Silver Bear, will lead a distinguished panel of international filmmakers and critics, underscoring Doha’s ambition to become a key cultural hub in the Middle East.

Rithy Panh: A Voice of Memory and History

Born in Phnom Kandal, Cambodia, Panh has spent his career documenting the traumas of the Khmer Rouge era while also exploring the resilience of everyday life. His feature “The Missing Picture” (2013) won the Un Certain Regard award at Cannes and earned an Oscar nomination for Best Foreign Language Film, cementing his reputation as a storyteller who blends personal narrative with collective memory.

More recently, Panh’s documentary “The King of the Children” secured the Berlin International Film Festival’s Silver Bear for Best Director, adding another feather to his already impressive international résumé.

First Doha Film Festival: A New Cultural Frontier

The festival’s inaugural edition marks Doha’s entry into the global cinematic conversation. Scheduled for November 20–28, the lineup will feature 75 films from over 30 countries, spanning feature dramas, documentaries, and shorts. The event will also host industry panels, masterclasses, and a dedicated section for Qatari and Gulf‑region filmmakers.

With a mission to foster cross‑cultural dialogue, the festival aims to spotlight under‑represented voices and encourage collaborations between Arab, African, Asian, and Latin American cinema communities.

Impact of Panh’s Leadership on the Jury

Panh’s appointment brings a seasoned perspective that balances artistic integrity with political sensitivity. His experience in navigating controversial historical subjects will be invaluable as the jury evaluates works that tackle social, political, and humanitarian themes.

Moreover, Panh’s reputation for mentorship—he has nurtured emerging filmmakers through the Cambodian Film Initiative—aligns with the festival’s goal of fostering the next generation of storytellers.

Looking Forward: Doha’s Cinematic Vision

By selecting Panh, the festival signals its commitment to high‑quality, socially relevant cinema. The event will also showcase Qatari talent through a “Qatar Spotlight” segment, offering local filmmakers a platform alongside international peers.

As the city prepares to host its first major film festival, stakeholders anticipate that the Doha Film Festival will become a catalyst for cultural tourism, academic discourse, and creative exchange throughout the Arab world.
