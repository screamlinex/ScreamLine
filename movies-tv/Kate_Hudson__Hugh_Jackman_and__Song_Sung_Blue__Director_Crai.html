Kate Hudson, Hugh Jackman and ‘Song Sung Blue’ Director Craig Brewer Salute Neil Diamond (and Call Him on FaceTime) at AFI Fest Premiere: ‘It’s a Dream Come True’

Summary: Neil Diamond didn’t make the trip to Hollywood for the world premiere of Song Sung Blue, the film about a Milwaukee couple who formed a tribute band to the “Sweet Caroline” crooner in the ’90s, but he still made an appearance on the red carpet via FaceTime. Diamond’s wife, Katie Diamond, rang up the 84‑year‑old legend, who sent his own “Happy Birthday” from his home studio, creating a memorable moment that blended Hollywood glamour with a touch of 90s nostalgia.

Red Carpet Rerun

When the AFI Fest premiere buzzed with anticipation, the usual Hollywood glitz was punctuated by an unexpected, heart‑warming digital cameo. Kate Hudson, Hugh Jackman, and director Craig Brewer stood beside their phones, all smiles as they connected with Diamond, who was perched in his familiar Brooklyn apartment. The live video feed, projected onto a giant screen at the venue, let the audience feel the warmth of an old‑school concert crowd from a distance.

Star‑Studded Cast

Hudson and Jackman, both veterans of musical biopics, shared laughs over the “sweet” tribute concept while discussing their roles in the film’s soundtrack. They praised the director’s approach to capturing the authentic feel of the 90s, noting how the film’s aesthetic mirrors the era’s iconic music videos and fashion trends. Their enthusiasm set the tone for an event that was as much a celebration of music history as it was a showcase of the new film.

Director’s Perspective

Craig Brewer, known for his gritty storytelling, explained how “Song Sung Blue” balances humor with heartfelt homage. He emphasized the importance of authenticity, citing the meticulous research into the couple’s original band’s setlists and stage presence. Brewer highlighted the film’s use of archival footage, which seamlessly integrates with newly shot scenes to create a time‑traveling vibe that honors Diamond’s legacy while keeping contemporary audiences engaged.

Diamond’s Digital Debut

Neil Diamond’s FaceTime appearance was more than a cameo; it was a full‑blown musical interlude. The legendary singer performed a brief rendition of “Sweet Caroline,” followed by a personalized message to the film’s creators and the audience. The clip, which went viral on social media, underscored Diamond’s willingness to adapt to modern platforms while staying true to the spirit of his classic hits.

Audience Reaction

Attendees, ranging from film critics to long‑time fans, were visibly moved by the unexpected blend of celebrity and intimacy. The live stream sparked a flurry of Instagram stories and tweets, with many praising the seamless integration of technology and tradition. As the screen faded to black, the applause echoed the enduring power of “Song Sung Blue” to bring people together, even across miles.
