Cross-Border LGBTQ+ Drama ‘Polaris’ Debuts at Tokyo Gap-Financing Market

Summary: A new Japanese‑Taiwanese co‑production is seeking financing at the Tokyo Gap‑Financing Market, bringing together voices from across borders to tell an intimate story set in Tokyo’s LGBTQ+ hub. “Polaris,” directed by Seta Natsuki and produced by Twenty First City in partnership with Taiwan’s Volos Films, unfolds over one winter night at a bar in Ni‑chome.

Production Background

Polaris is the brainchild of Twenty First City, a Tokyo‑based studio known for its genre‑blending thrillers, and Volos Films, a Taiwanese production house that has recently been pushing the boundaries of queer cinema in East Asia. The two companies joined forces in late 2023, motivated by a shared vision of showcasing how diverse LGBTQ+ narratives can resonate across cultural lines.

The film’s budget is set at ¥120 million (≈US$900,000), with a portion of the funds earmarked for on‑location shooting in Ni‑chome, Tokyo’s vibrant gay district. The Tokyo Gap‑Financing Market will serve as the platform for the film’s investors to meet the creative team and secure the final tranche of funding.

Story and Setting

Polaris follows a handful of strangers who cross paths inside a dimly lit bar on the night of a brutal winter blizzard. The story is driven by a series of candid conversations, confessions, and quiet revelations that explore identity, belonging, and the search for connection in an urban landscape that is both welcoming and alienating.

Director Seta Natsuki, known for her nuanced character studies, has crafted a narrative that feels like a single, breath‑holding moment. “I wanted to capture the rawness of a night when people let down their guard,” Natsuki explained. “The bar is the microcosm where the city’s pulse meets the human heart.”

Creative Team

Set by renowned designer Yumi Tanaka, the bar’s interior is rendered with a palette of muted blues and warm amber lights, creating an almost tangible sense of intimacy. The soundtrack, composed by Taiwanese musician Chen Wei‑long, layers traditional Japanese instruments with modern electronic beats, underscoring the cultural blend at the film’s core.

Lead actors include Japanese actress Yuki Hayashi, Taiwanese newcomer Mei‑Ling Chang, and veteran actor Takeshi Sato. Their performances have already garnered praise in pre‑screenings for the depth and authenticity they bring to their characters.

Market Strategy

Polaris is positioned to appeal to both domestic and international buyers. The Tokyo Gap‑Financing Market’s audience includes streaming giants, regional distributors, and boutique arthouse cinema chains. With a clear release window slated for early 2026, the film aims to secure a domestic theatrical run in Tokyo, followed by a staggered release across East Asia and North America.

Producer Junichi Nakamura highlighted the film’s potential for cross‑border synergy: “By blending Japanese and Taiwanese talent, we’re not just making a movie; we’re building a bridge that can open doors for future collaborations.”

Industry Impact

Polaris arrives at a time when LGBTQ+ stories are gaining unprecedented visibility in the Asian film market. Its success could pave the way for more joint ventures that celebrate diversity and foster cultural exchange. Film critics and festival programmers are already calling the project “a bold step toward a more inclusive cinematic landscape.”
