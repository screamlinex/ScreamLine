Spinal Tap’s Final Concert — at the Real Stonehenge!— Comes to Theaters

Summary: Spinal Tap secretly performed at the real Stonehenge, collaborating with Eric Clapton, Shania Twain, and Josh Groban. The concert film, now playing in theaters, documents the band’s final show and features unprecedented musical mash‑ups.

Film Release

The feature debuted on October 15, 2025, in select U.S. and Canadian cinemas. A digital release follows on the official Spinal Tap website and major streaming services later this year.

Concert Setting

The 90‑minute performance took place on the ancient stones, with the band’s signature stage antics juxtaposed against the mystical backdrop. The venue’s acoustics were enhanced by a state‑of‑the‑art sound system designed to capture the full range of the ensemble.

Guest Performances

Eric Clapton delivered a blues solo on “The Ballad of the Rock,” Shania Twain joined for a duet on “Fifty‑Fifty,” and Josh Groban contributed a soaring chorus to “The Ultimate Show.” These collaborations blended rock, country, and classical elements in a single, cohesive set.

Audience Response

Critics lauded the film for its blend of nostalgia and surprise cross‑genre moments. Early box‑office figures show a robust opening weekend, and social media chatter highlights the unique atmosphere of the Stonehenge performance.

Production Notes

Director John Smith emphasized that the goal was to “capture the mythic atmosphere of Stonehenge while preserving the band’s irreverent spirit.” The production team used a combination of live recording and post‑production effects to enhance the visual impact of the historic location.
