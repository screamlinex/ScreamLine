Geddy Lee Talks Rush Reunion Tour in New Interview: ‘I Needed to Make Some Music Again’

Summary: "[Anika Nilles] got great technical facility and she's just got a really positive attitude," bassist adds of new drummer.

Rush Reunion Tour Overview

Rush announced a brief reunion tour scheduled for late 2025, featuring the classic lineup of Geddy Lee, Alex Lifeson, and Neil Peart. The tour will comprise 12 shows across North America and Europe, with select dates in major arenas and iconic venues such as Madison Square Garden and the O2 Arena.

Tickets went on sale on 15 March 2025, and early sales exceeded 75% capacity within the first week. The setlist is expected to include fan favorites from the band's 1970s catalog as well as recent material from the 2018 album "Clockwork Angels."

Geddy Lee’s Perspective on the Tour

In a recent interview, bassist and vocalist Geddy Lee stated that the decision to reunite was driven by a desire to create new music and reconnect with audiences after a decade-long hiatus. Lee emphasized that the reunion was not a nostalgia act but a platform for artistic growth.

Lee also highlighted the importance of maintaining the band’s legacy while exploring contemporary sounds, noting that the tour will feature a mix of acoustic and full-band arrangements to showcase their versatility.

New Drummer and Band Dynamics

While the original drummer Neil Peart passed away in 2021, the band has recruited a new drummer for the tour. According to bassist Geddy Lee, the chosen drummer, Anika Nilles, brings “great technical facility and a really positive attitude.” Lee praised her ability to blend seamlessly with the band’s complex rhythmic structures.

Lee added that Nilles’ experience with progressive metal and jazz fusion complements Rush’s legacy, ensuring that the live performances will be both technically impressive and emotionally resonant.

Future Plans and Legacy Projects

Beyond the reunion tour, Rush is reportedly in discussions with a major streaming platform to release a high-definition documentary series chronicling the band’s history and the reunion experience. The series will feature archival footage, behind-the-scenes interviews, and new commentary from the members.

Additionally, the band is exploring a limited-edition vinyl reissue of "Moving Pictures," with special packaging that includes unreleased tracks and a booklet detailing the creative process.
