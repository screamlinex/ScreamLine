Watch Thundercat, Remi Wolf Perform ‘Children of the Baked Potato’ on ‘Colbert’

Summary: Thundercat and Remi Wolf performed their newly released single “Children of the Baked Potato” on The Late Show with Stephen Colbert. The track, dropped in September, honors the iconic Los Angeles jazz club, Baked Potato.

Performance Highlights

The duo delivered a dynamic, high‑energy rendition on the Colbert stage, blending Thundercat’s virtuosic bass lines with Remi Wolf’s bright vocal hooks. The set included a brief instrumental jam that showcased the musicians’ improvisational skills, drawing cheers from the live audience.

Song Background

Released in September, “Children of the Baked Potato” is a collaborative effort between bassist Thundercat (Stephen Lee) and pop vocalist Remi Wolf (Emily M. W. Smith). The song pays tribute to the legendary Baked Potato club in West Hollywood, known for its intimate jazz performances and as a gathering place for artists across genres.

Artists’ Statements

In a pre‑airing interview, Thundercat noted, “The Baked Potato has always been a source of inspiration. We wanted to capture its spirit in a way that feels fresh.” Remi Wolf added, “It was a dream to play the club’s vibe on national TV and share it with new listeners.”

Audience Reception

Viewers praised the performance for its authentic jazz feel and the seamless chemistry between the two musicians. Social media buzz highlighted the track’s catchy hook and the respectful homage to the club’s legacy.
