See Lana Del Rey Cover Neil Young’s ‘The Needle and the Damage Done’ at Harvest Moon Concert

Summary: “I thought we’d get started with a little bit of Neil,” singer Lana Del Rey said before launching into her own Harvest classic at a charity concert.

Concert Overview

The Harvest Moon Concert, a benefit event for mental‑health charities, opened on a humid Saturday night in Los Angeles. With a sold‑out crowd of 3,200, the lineup featured a mix of contemporary pop, indie rock, and tribute performances.

Lana Del Rey’s Performance

Del Rey took the stage in a black lace dress, her signature smoky makeup, and began with a brief spoken introduction. “I thought we’d get started with a little bit of Neil,” she announced, setting a reflective tone for the evening.

She then delivered a haunting rendition of Neil Young’s “The Needle and the Damage Done,” a song that has become emblematic of the opioid crisis. The performance was noted for its minimalistic arrangement, featuring only her voice and a single acoustic guitar.

Neil Young Tribute

Del Rey’s cover was praised for its emotional depth and faithful adherence to the original melody. Critics highlighted her ability to convey the song’s sorrow while maintaining her own vocal style. The set was followed by a brief discussion about the song’s relevance to contemporary social issues.

Audience Reaction

Attendees responded with a standing ovation after the final chord, and several audience members posted live reactions on social media, tagging the event and the charity. The crowd’s engagement was measured in a 97% positive feedback rate in post‑event surveys.

Charity Impact

All proceeds from the concert, including ticket sales and merchandise, were directed to the Mental Health America Foundation. The event raised $125,000, surpassing the initial target by 20%. Del Rey’s participation was credited with boosting ticket sales and increasing media coverage for the cause.
