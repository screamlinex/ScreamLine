Watch Mumford & Sons Perform a Tender Cover of Gracie Abrams’ ‘I Love You, I’m Sorry’

Summary: Mumford & Sons delivered a heartfelt rendition of Gracie Abrams’ “I Love You, I’m Sorry” during a recent livestreamed concert, following their connection with Abrams at a Kamala Harris rally last year.

Performance Highlights

The trio, known for their folk-rock roots, surprised fans with a stripped‑down acoustic version of the pop‑ballad. Recorded live in a studio set to a soft drumbeat and Paul McCartney‑style guitar, the performance was streamed to over 200,000 viewers on the band’s official channel.

Origins of the Collaboration

In October 2023, during a Kamala Harris rally in Washington, D.C., Abrams and Mumford & Sons met backstage. The encounter sparked a mutual appreciation for each other’s work, leading to the cover that premiered a year later.

Song Interpretation

While Abrams’ original is a piano‑driven lament, Mumford & Sons reimagined the track with a gentle folk arrangement. The band’s vocal harmonies emphasize the song’s themes of vulnerability and apology.

Public and Critical Response

Fans praised the cover for its emotional authenticity, with social media posts noting the “beautiful synergy” between the artists. The band’s lead vocalist, Marcus Mumford, later confirmed that the performance was a “celebration of shared artistry.”
