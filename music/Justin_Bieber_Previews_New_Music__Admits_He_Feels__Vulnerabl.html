Justin Bieber Previews New Music, Admits He Feels ‘Vulnerable As Hell’ in Twitch Stream

Summary: Justin Bieber conducted a weeklong Twitch livestream series, sharing studio footage, previewing unreleased tracks, and openly discussing his feelings of vulnerability and mental health challenges.

Twitch Livestream Series

Bieber streamed daily on Twitch from Monday to Friday, attracting an average of 120,000 concurrent viewers. Each session featured behind‑the‑scenes footage of his recording studio, live Q&A segments, and interactive polls that let fans influence setlists.

New Music Teasers

During the streams, the artist played short clips of two forthcoming songs: a melodic pop‑R&B track titled “Eclipse” and a more experimental house‑influenced track called “Nebula.” He described the new material as “raw and emotionally charged.”

Personal Reflections

In a candid moment, Bieber admitted, “I feel vulnerable as hell,” explaining that the pressure of constant public scrutiny and his recent health setbacks have intensified his self‑awareness. He emphasized the importance of seeking professional help and maintaining a support network.

Fan Engagement

Fans were able to submit song requests and ask personal questions via chat. Bieber responded to more than 300 messages, offering advice on coping with fame and encouraging viewers to prioritize mental health.

Future Plans

Following the livestreams, Bieber confirmed an upcoming EP release in August and announced a short tour later this year. He stated that the new music will reflect the themes of vulnerability and resilience discussed during the streams.
