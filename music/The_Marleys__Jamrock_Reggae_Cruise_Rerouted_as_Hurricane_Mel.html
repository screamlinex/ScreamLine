The Marleys’ Jamrock Reggae Cruise Rerouted as Hurricane Melissa Nears Jamaica

Summary: The Marleys’ Jamrock Reggae Cruise, set to depart from Miami, has been rerouted due to Hurricane Melissa approaching Jamaica. The original itinerary included stops in Montego Bay and Ocho Rios.

Original Itinerary

The cruise was scheduled to leave Miami on Friday, 12 September, with a planned arrival in Montego Bay, Jamaica, on Saturday, 13 September, followed by a port call in Ocho Rios on Sunday, 14 September. Passengers had booked a 7‑night sailing experience featuring live reggae performances and cultural excursions.

Hurricane Threat

Hurricane Melissa, a Category 4 storm, is projected to make landfall in central Jamaica within 48 hours. The National Hurricane Center has issued a hurricane warning for the region, prompting maritime authorities to reassess shipping routes.

Rerouting Decision

In coordination with the U.S. Coast Guard and the Royal Caribbean Group, the cruise line announced a new schedule that will divert the vessel to the Bahamas. The revised itinerary will include a stop in Nassau on the same day of departure, with a return to Miami on Thursday, 18 September.

Passenger Impact

All passengers were notified via email and the cruise line’s mobile app. The company assures no additional costs for the change and will provide complimentary lounge access and onboard entertainment during the altered route. Reservations can be modified for future sailings at no charge.
