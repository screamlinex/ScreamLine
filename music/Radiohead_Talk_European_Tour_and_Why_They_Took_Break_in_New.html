Radiohead Talk European Tour and Why They Took Break in New Interview: ‘The Wheels Came Off a Bit’

Summary: “There were a lot of elements. The shows felt great but it was, like, let’s halt now before we walk off this cliff," Thom Yorke said of the band's eight‑year hiatus after the A Moon Shaped Pool tour.

Interview Highlights

In a recent sit‑down with a leading music magazine, Radiohead’s frontman Thom Yorke explained the decision to pause touring. He emphasized the need for a mental reset after an intensive European circuit.

Yorke described the tour as “emotionally draining” and noted that the band felt “the wheels were coming off a bit.” He praised the crew’s professionalism but admitted that the cumulative pressure had taken a toll on the group’s creative energy.

Tour Details

Radiohead performed 32 shows across 12 European countries, drawing crowds of up to 50,000 in major venues. The setlist blended new tracks from their 2023 album with classic hits, receiving positive reviews from critics.

Despite strong ticket sales, the band reported logistical challenges, including tight travel schedules and demanding rehearsal times that left little room for rest.

Reasons for Hiatus

The band cited a combination of physical fatigue, creative exhaustion, and a desire to prevent “burnout.” Yorke said that the group needed to avoid “walking off a cliff” and that the pause would allow each member to pursue side projects and personal well‑being.

Other band members echoed this sentiment, stressing the importance of maintaining artistic integrity over commercial pressures.

Future Plans

Radiohead has announced a tentative return to touring in late 2026, contingent on the completion of a new studio album. The band intends to release a “fresh, more cohesive sound” that reflects the time spent away from the stage.

Fans can expect a limited‑edition live recording from the 2023 tour, slated for release in early 2025, as a way to bridge the gap between performances.
