Radiohead Talk European Tour and Why They Took Break in New Interview: ‘The Wheels Came Off a Bit’

Summary: Radiohead’s eight‑year hiatus followed the 2016–2017 “A Moon Shaped Pool” tour, as the band decided to pause before “walking off a cliff.” Thom Yorke described the break as a necessary reset for the group’s creative and personal balance.

Tour Highlights

Radiohead’s final shows of the “A Moon Shaped Pool” tour were held across Europe, with sold‑out venues in London, Paris, and Berlin. The setlists blended new material with fan favorites, and the band’s stage production was noted for its minimalist yet striking visual design.

Reasons for the Hiatus

During a recent interview, Thom Yorke explained that the band’s “wheels came off a bit” after the tour’s rigorous schedule. He cited physical fatigue, creative exhaustion, and the need for personal space as key factors in their decision to step back.

Band Dynamics

Members reported that the tour’s intensity strained interpersonal relationships. The group agreed that a pause would allow them to recharge, re‑evaluate their collaborative process, and prevent long‑term burnout.

Future Outlook

While no new album has been announced, Radiohead has indicated that the break will not be permanent. The band plans to return to the studio in 2026, with a focus on fresh material and a more flexible touring schedule.
