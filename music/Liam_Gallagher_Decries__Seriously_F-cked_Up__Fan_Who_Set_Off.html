Liam Gallagher Decries ‘Seriously F-cked Up’ Fan Who Set Off Flares at Oasis’ Australia Show

Summary: Liam Gallagher criticized a fan who launched flares into the floor area during Oasis’s Australia tour, calling the incident “seriously f‑cked up.” Video footage captured two flares being ignited and tossed toward the stage as the band performed “Champagne Supernova.”

Incident Details

At the Adelaide venue on Thursday, a spectator ignited two flares in the front‑row seating and hurled them toward the stage during Oasis’s set. The flares landed in the floor area, creating a brief fire hazard and forcing the band to pause for safety checks. Security personnel and fire crews responded promptly, extinguishing the flares and evacuating a small section of the audience.

Gallagher’s Response

Following the incident, Liam Gallagher posted a statement on social media, calling the fan’s actions “dangerously irresponsible” and “a serious breach of public safety.” He emphasized that the band’s priority was the safety of the crowd and that no injuries were reported. Gallagher urged authorities to take action against the individual.

Legal and Safety Measures

Police have opened a criminal investigation into the incident. The venue’s management has announced a review of security protocols, including increased surveillance and stricter bag‑search procedures. Fire safety officials have also issued a reminder that the use of flares or any incendiary device is prohibited at all live events in Australia.
