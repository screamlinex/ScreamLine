Drake Launches Appeal, Hopes to Revive Defamation Lawsuit Against UMG

Summary: Drake has filed an appeal to reinstate his defamation lawsuit against Universal Music Group (UMG). The original case was dismissed by a judge who ruled that Kendrick Lamar’s alleged defamatory remarks in the song “Not Like Us” amounted to a “nonactionable opinion.”

Background of the Lawsuit

In 2022, Drake sued UMG, alleging that the lyrics in Kendrick Lamar’s track disparaged him and damaged his reputation. Drake claimed the statements were false, defamatory, and made with actual malice, seeking damages and a retraction.

Judge’s Dismissal Rationale

The presiding judge dismissed the case on the basis that the lyrics were protected opinions rather than factual assertions. The ruling emphasized that opinions, even if harsh, are generally not actionable under defamation law unless they imply undisclosed defamatory facts.

Appeal Filing Details

Drake’s legal team argues that the judge misapplied the "nonactionable opinion" doctrine, pointing to contextual clues that suggest factual claims about Drake’s conduct. The appeal will be heard in the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit.

Potential Legal Implications

If the appellate court overturns the dismissal, it could broaden the scope of what constitutes actionable defamation in the music industry. The case may prompt a reevaluation of how lyrical content is treated under First Amendment protections.

Next Steps and Timeline

The appellate court has granted a brief period for both parties to file written briefs. A hearing date has not yet been set, but the court is expected to rule within the next six months. Drake’s team remains hopeful that a reversal will allow the case to proceed to trial.
