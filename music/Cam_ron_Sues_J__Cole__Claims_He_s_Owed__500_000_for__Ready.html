Cam’ron Sues J. Cole, Claims He’s Owed $500,000 for ‘Ready ’24’

Summary: Cam'ron has filed a lawsuit against rapper J. Cole, alleging the Brooklyn‑born artist owes $500,000 for the planned collaboration on the forthcoming “Ready ’24” project. The suit claims that Cole backed out of an agreement to appear on a single and on Cam’ron’s podcast, breaching contractual terms.

Legal Filing

The lawsuit was filed on Monday in the Manhattan U.S. District Court. Cam'ron, represented by attorney T. P. Johnson, accuses J. Cole of violating a written contract dated February 15, 2024.

Financial Claim

Cam'ron seeks $500,000 in damages, citing the agreed-upon payment for J. Cole’s performance on the “Ready ’24” single and for participation in the podcast series. The claim also includes a request for interest and legal fees.

Collaboration Terms

The contract specified that J. Cole would record a verse for Cam'ron’s lead single and appear in a two‑episode podcast segment discussing the project’s creative direction. According to Cam'ron’s filing, the rapper failed to deliver the verse and declined the podcast invitation after the recording date was set.

Response

J. Cole’s management has not yet issued a formal statement. In the absence of a response, the lawsuit remains pending while both parties negotiate a potential settlement.
