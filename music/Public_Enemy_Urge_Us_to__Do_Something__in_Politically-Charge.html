Public Enemy Urge Us to ‘Do Something’ in Politically-Charged Music Video

Summary: Public Enemy’s single “The Hits Just Keep on Comin’,” from the album Black Sky Over the Projects: Apartment 2025, is accompanied by a politically charged music video starring a U.S. water polo Olympian. The clip urges viewers to take action on social and political issues.

Release and Album Context

Released on October 5, 2025, “The Hits Just Keep on Comin’” serves as the lead single for Public Enemy’s latest studio effort, Black Sky Over the Projects: Apartment 2025. The album continues the group’s tradition of blending hard‑hitting hip‑hop with incisive social commentary.

Music Video Themes

The video opens with stark footage of urban decay, intercut with scenes of the band performing on a rooftop overlooking the city. It intersperses archival protest footage from the 1990s with contemporary images of police and civic unrest. The central message calls listeners to “do something” rather than remain passive.

Featured Athlete

The clip stars a U.S. water polo Olympian who appears in a brief but powerful monologue about athletes’ responsibility to engage in civic life. The athlete’s on‑screen presence underscores the link between sports, national pride, and political activism.

Reception

Critics praised the video’s bold visuals and timely message, citing it as a “relevant reminder of the role art plays in social movements.” Fans on social media have echoed this sentiment, with hashtags related to the song trending on Twitter and Instagram.
