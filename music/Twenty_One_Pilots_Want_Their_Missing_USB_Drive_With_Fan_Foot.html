Twenty One Pilots Want Their Missing USB Drive With Fan Footage: It Carries ‘Significant Meaning’

Summary: Twenty One Pilots lost a USB drive containing rare fan footage during a Los Angeles show, prompting the band to publicly request assistance from fans in locating the item.

The USB Drive’s Significance

The drive allegedly holds a compilation of behind‑the‑scenes videos and unreleased performances, some of which were recorded exclusively for the band’s dedicated fan community. Lead vocalist Tyler Joseph described it as “a piece of our shared history that carries significant meaning” for both the band and its audience.

The Incident at the L.A. Show

During a 2025 concert at the Hollywood Palladium, a security breach occurred when a member of the crowd reportedly removed the USB drive from the backstage area. Despite an immediate search by the venue’s staff, the device was not recovered before the show’s conclusion.

Band’s Response & Call for Fans

Following the loss, Tyler Joseph posted a short message on social media, urging fans to “keep an eye out” for the drive and to contact the band if they find it. The post included a photo of the USB’s label and a brief explanation of its importance.

What Fans Can Do

Fans who encounter a small black USB drive with the Twenty One Pilots logo are asked to contact the band’s official support email or send a direct message to the official Instagram account. The band’s management will verify the item’s authenticity and offer a reward for its safe return.
