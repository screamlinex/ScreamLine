Chappell Roan Will Induct Cyndi Lauper Into Rock & Roll Hall of Fame

Summary: Chappell Roan will induct Cyndi Lauper into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame at this year’s ceremony, joined by a star‑filled roster of guests that includes The Killers.

Induction Announcement

The Rock & Roll Hall of Fame announced that Chappell Roan will deliver the induction speech for pop icon Cyndi Lauper. The ceremony, scheduled for Sunday night, will also feature performances by the Killers, among other high‑profile acts.

Cyndi Lauper’s Career Highlights

Since her breakthrough with “Girls Just Want to Have Fun” in 1983, Lauper has earned five Grammy Awards and sold more than 45 million records worldwide. Her eclectic style and outspoken advocacy have cemented her legacy as a defining figure in 1980s pop.

Chappell Roan’s Role

Roan, a longtime collaborator and friend of Lauper, will introduce her and share personal anecdotes that underscore her impact on the music industry. He has previously served as a host for several award shows, bringing a blend of humor and respect to the stage.

Guest Lineup

In addition to Roan, the ceremony will feature the Killers as performing guests. Other confirmed attendees include industry leaders, musicians, and former inductees, creating a diverse and celebratory atmosphere for the event.
