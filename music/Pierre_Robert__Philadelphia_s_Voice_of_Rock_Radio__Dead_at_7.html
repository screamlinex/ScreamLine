Pierre Robert, Philadelphia’s Voice of Rock Radio, Dead at 70

Summary: Pierre Robert, Philadelphia’s longtime rock radio icon, died on Sunday at the age of 70. Known for his warm, welcoming timbre and signature “Greetings, citizens!” greeting, Robert hosted the station’s flagship morning show for over three decades and was a lifelong fan of the Grateful Dead.

Career Highlights

Robert began broadcasting in 1986 at WMMR, quickly earning a reputation as the station’s “Voice of Rock.” He hosted the 6 a.m.–10 a.m. block for 32 years, consistently ranking among the top-rated programs in the Philadelphia market.

In addition to his morning show, Robert filled in as a weekend host, produced specialty segments on classic rock history, and served as a spokesperson for the station’s charitable initiatives.

Signature Style

His distinct delivery—marked by a clear, friendly tone—made him a trusted presence for listeners. The “Greetings, citizens!” opening became a cultural touchstone, often replayed on local talk shows and in online playlists.

Robert’s deep knowledge of rock lore and his regular shout-outs to the Grateful Dead resonated with a multigenerational audience, cementing his status as a local legend.

Legacy and Impact

Peers and fans alike praised Robert for fostering a sense of community among rock enthusiasts. He mentored numerous on-air personalities and championed emerging artists through dedicated “New Music” segments.

Following his death, the station announced a monthly tribute segment, and local musicians paid homage at a memorial concert held in his honor.

Final Days

Robert’s passing was announced by station management after he was found unresponsive at his home. He had been battling a chronic illness for several years but had continued to appear on-air until the week before his death.

The Philadelphia community has organized several memorial events, and the station plans to dedicate a new “Pierre Robert” playlist to keep his memory alive.
