Dawes Enlist Brad Paisley, Brandon Flowers, More for L.A. Fires Anniversary Benefit

Summary: Dawes, who lost their home to the Altadena L.A. Fires, will headline a benefit concert on the one‑year anniversary of the blaze, joined by Brad Paisley, Brandon Flowers, Jackson Browne, Jenny Lewis, and other artists. The event aims to raise funds for victims and support community rebuilding efforts.

Band’s Response to the Fires

Dawes’ frontman, Adam MacDougall, confirmed that the band’s Altadena house was destroyed during the 2023 wildfire. The loss has galvanized the group to use their platform for relief efforts, pledging a portion of concert proceeds to the Altadena Community Fund.

Benefit Concert Details

The concert will take place on Saturday, August 17th at the historic Los Angeles Theatre. Tickets are priced between $35 and $150, with VIP packages including meet‑and‑greet access. All revenue will be directed to wildfire survivors and reconstruction projects.

Featured Guests

Alongside Dawes, country icon Brad Paisley and The Killers frontman Brandon Flowers will perform. The lineup also includes singer‑songwriter Jackson Browne, indie rocker Jenny Lewis, and a surprise appearance by the Altadena Community Choir.

Community Impact and Outreach

Local officials highlighted the event’s role in boosting morale and providing much-needed financial support. The benefit will also showcase educational resources on wildfire prevention and sustainable living practices.

How to Contribute

Individuals unable to attend can donate directly through the event’s official website. The platform will also distribute a portion of ticket sales to the L.A. Fire & Rescue Volunteer Fund, ensuring long‑term community resilience.
