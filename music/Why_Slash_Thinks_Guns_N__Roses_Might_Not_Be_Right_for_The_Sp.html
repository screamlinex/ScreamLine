Why Slash Thinks Guns N' Roses Might Not Be Right for The Sphere

Summary: Kevin Winter of Getty Images captured Slash as he explains why he believes Guns N’ Roses may not be the best fit for the Sphere, the newly opened multi‑purpose arena in Las Vegas.

Slash’s Take on the Sphere

During a recent interview, Slash expressed uncertainty about performing at the Sphere. He noted that the venue’s advanced acoustics and immersive technology require a level of production that may not align with the band’s current setup.

Why the Sphere Matters

The Sphere is a 18,000‑seat venue that offers 360‑degree sound and a massive LED display. Its design prioritizes high‑definition audio and visual experiences, which can be demanding for touring acts.

Band Dynamics and Performance Needs

Guns N’ Roses has undergone lineup changes in recent years, and the current members may lack the depth needed to fully exploit the Sphere’s capabilities. Slash highlighted that a more robust touring ensemble would be essential.

Potential Alternatives

Slash suggested that acts with larger, more seasoned touring crews—such as Aerosmith or AC/DC—might be better suited to showcase the Sphere’s features. He also mentioned that the band could explore a scaled‑down show if they decide to proceed.
