Sevdaliza Unleashes Her Inner ‘Heroina’ With Karol G, Kenia Os, Tokischa on New LP

Summary: "Heroina is about wanting to be free, and at the same time feeling how limited that freedom can be," Sevdaliza said. "That tension between surrender and resistance became the core of this album."

Album Release and Production

Sevdaliza’s third studio album, Heroina, dropped on November 7, 2024, following a six‑month promotional campaign that included a surprise single featuring Colombian reggaetón star Karol G. The record was recorded across studios in Los Angeles, Madrid, and São Paulo, with production handled by Sevdaliza herself alongside collaborators Lido, Teo Halm, and the experimental duo Tuxedo.

Notable Collaborations

In addition to Karol G, the album showcases guest appearances by rising Latin pop figure Kenia Os on the track “Alma”, and Venezuelan rapper Tokischa on the club‑ready "Fuego". These collaborations blend Sevdaliza’s avant‑garde sound with contemporary Latin rhythms, expanding the album’s sonic palette and broadening its audience reach.

Musical Themes and Style

Sevdaliza’s signature blend of trip‑hop, electronica, and soul is pushed further in Heroina, with layered vocal samples and cinematic synth layers. Lyrically, the album tackles autonomy, identity, and the paradox of longing for liberation while acknowledging societal constraints. Tracks like “Cadenas” and “Luz” feature haunting piano motifs that underscore the emotional tension highlighted in the artist’s own quote.

Critical Reception and Impact

Critics have praised Heroina for its cohesive narrative and bold genre experimentation. Pitchfork described the record as “a fearless fusion of global sounds that still feels intimately personal.” Commercially, the album debuted at number 12 on the Billboard Top R&B/Hip‑Hop Albums chart and has already amassed over 3 million streams across streaming platforms within the first month.
