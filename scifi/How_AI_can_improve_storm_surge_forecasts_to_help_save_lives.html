How AI can improve storm surge forecasts to help save lives

Summary: Accurate storm surge predictions are critical for giving coastal residents time to evacuate and giving emergency responders time to prepare.

Current Challenges in Storm Surge Forecasting

Traditional models rely on limited observational data and simplified physics, which can lead to under‑ or over‑estimates of surge height, especially in complex coastal geometries.

AI-Driven Data Assimilation

Machine‑learning algorithms can ingest satellite imagery, buoy data, and radar measurements in real time, filling gaps and correcting biases in the raw data before it enters the forecast system.

Deep‑Learning Surge Models

Neural networks trained on historical storm events learn nonlinear relationships between atmospheric drivers and coastal response, enabling faster, more accurate predictions that can be updated every few minutes.

Improved Decision Support for Evacuations

With higher‑confidence forecasts, authorities can issue targeted evacuation orders, allocate resources efficiently, and reduce the risk of unnecessary displacement or delayed sheltering.
