Watch 2 Russian cosmonauts spacewalk outside the ISS today

Summary: Cosmonauts Sergey Ryzhikov and Alexey Zubritsky will conduct a spacewalk outside the International Space Station today (Oct. 28), and you can watch the action live.

Mission Overview

Today’s EVA, the 17th of the year for the Russian segment of the ISS, will take place during the early afternoon shift. Ryzhikov, commander of the Soyuz MS‑26 crew, and Zubritsky, a seasoned long‑duration flight engineer, will step out of the Zvezda service module to perform a series of maintenance tasks on the exterior of the station’s solar arrays and support equipment.

Objectives & Tasks

The primary goal is to replace a malfunctioning panel on the European Columbus module’s power system and to install new hardware on the ISS’s Russian “Zvezda” module. The crew will also retrieve and analyze debris that has accumulated on the station’s outer surfaces, providing data that will help engineers plan future maintenance and avoid potential collisions.

Live Streaming & Viewer Experience

The spacewalk will be broadcast live by Roscosmos on the official ISS Live channel. Viewers can tune in via the Russian Space Agency’s website, the ISS Live app, or the ISS Live YouTube channel. The feed will include live commentary in Russian with English subtitles, giving fans a behind‑the‑scenes look at the cosmonauts’ work and the station’s dynamic environment.

Technical Highlights

During the EVA, Ryzhikov and Zubritsky will use the newly updated “Pankh” extravehicular suits, which incorporate advanced thermal insulation and improved mobility. The EVA’s duration is expected to last approximately 4 hours, with a planned return to the airlock for a safety check before re‑entry into the station’s cabin.
