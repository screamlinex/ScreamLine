'Once Upon a Time in Space': How to watch the latest BBC space documentary series from anywhere in the world

Summary: Follow the space program since the moon landing in the four‑part documentary “Once Upon a Time in Space.” Here’s how to watch it from anywhere on the planet.

1. Where It’s Officially Available

The BBC releases the series on its own streaming platform, BBC iPlayer, in the UK and on BBC Studios’ global streaming service, BBC Studios’ “BBC iPlayer Worldwide” in select markets. In the US, the show streams on the streaming‑on‑demand service “BBC America” and “BritBox” as part of the BBC’s subscription package. Other regions may have agreements with local broadcasters such as SBS (Australia) or TF1 (France).

2. Using the BBC iPlayer (UK‑only)

To watch via BBC iPlayer you need a UK IP address and a valid UK bank account or a UK‑based credit card to verify your identity. Once logged in, the series is available in full for free to UK residents. The iPlayer app works on Smart TVs, streaming sticks, mobile devices, and web browsers.

3. International Streaming Options

In most other countries the series is streamed through the BBC’s international portal, which is part of the “BBC Studios” catalogue. Subscriptions are available on a monthly or annual basis, and many devices—Apple TV, Amazon Fire, Roku, Android TV, and iOS—support the app. If you live in a country not yet covered, check the BBC website for the latest partner list or look for “BBC America” on local platforms.

4. Workarounds for Restricted Regions

When the series isn’t officially available in your country, you can:

• Subscribe to a reputable VPN that offers servers in the UK or US and stream through BBC iPlayer or BBC America. Remember to enable a “private” mode to avoid tracking.

• Use the “BBC Studios” streaming service’s free trial (if available) to watch all four episodes in one go.

• Check for free‑to‑air broadcasts or public‑access channels that occasionally air BBC documentaries—this is rare but worth searching local listings.
