PBS celebrates 25 years of the ISS with NOVA's 'Operation Space Station' on Nov. 5

Summary: This intimate look at the design, building, and operation of the ISS airs on PBS Nov. 5 and Nov. 12.

Celebrating a Quarter‑Century in Space

PBS marks 25 years of the International Space Station with a special NOVA episode, “Operation Space Station.” The series chronicles the station’s evolution from a symbolic diplomatic bridge to a bustling laboratory that has hosted dozens of experiments and crews from every nation.

Designing a Floating City

The episode takes viewers behind the scenes of the ISS’s modular architecture, showing how six separate laboratories, solar arrays, and living quarters were engineered to fit together in orbit. Engineers explain the challenges of working with microgravity, radiation, and limited resources.

Building the Station Piece by Piece

Through archival footage and expert interviews, viewers see the step‑by‑step construction process: launching the first module in 1998, attaching new segments, and adapting the station’s structure for future missions. The narrative highlights key milestones, such as the first spacewalk to install the Zvezda module and the installation of the Canadarm2 robotic arm.

Operating in Orbit: Daily Life and Science

The program provides an inside look at the routine of ISS crews, from sleeping in 4‑inch‑high bunk beds to conducting experiments in microgravity that range from human biology to advanced materials science. Viewers learn how data collected on the station feeds back to research labs on Earth and informs future deep‑space missions.
