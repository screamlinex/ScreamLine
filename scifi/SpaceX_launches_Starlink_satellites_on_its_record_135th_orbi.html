SpaceX launches Starlink satellites on its record 135th orbital mission of the year

Summary: SpaceX launched another batch of its Starlink satellites from California today (Oct. 25). It was the 135th orbital launch of 2025 for the company, setting a new record for the most launches in a single year.

Launch Details

The Falcon 9 rocket, powered by the upgraded Raptor engines, lifted off from Space Launch Complex 40 at Cape Canaveral. A total of 60 Starlink V2 satellites were deployed into a 550‑km, 53° inclination orbit, following the standard payload drop sequence that SpaceX has refined over the past year.

Mission Significance

With 135 launches, SpaceX has surpassed its own previous record of 130 in 2024, underscoring the company’s relentless cadence of satellite deployment. The new V2 payloads feature larger solar arrays and improved beamforming, promising higher bandwidth and lower latency for global broadband coverage.

Starlink Network Growth

Adding 60 more nodes brings the constellation to over 4,000 active satellites, a 12% increase from the end of last year. This expansion is critical for maintaining service quality as user demand in both developed and underserved markets continues to climb.

Future Outlook

SpaceX plans to continue launching 20–30 satellites per month through 2026, with the goal of reaching 12,000 satellites by 2030. The company’s focus on reusable rockets and rapid manufacturing will keep launch costs near $2.5 million per vehicle, making the vision of global broadband a tangible reality.
