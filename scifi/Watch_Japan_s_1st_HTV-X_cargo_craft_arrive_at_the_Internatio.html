Watch Japan's 1st HTV-X cargo craft arrive at the International Space Station today

Summary: Japan’s first HTV-X cargo spacecraft successfully docked with the International Space Station today, Oct. 28, marking a milestone in the nation’s space logistics program. The automated delivery of scientific experiments, spare parts, and crew supplies will support ISS operations for the coming year, while showcasing advances in Japanese space technology and international cooperation.

Mission Overview

HTV-X, the successor to the H-II Transfer Vehicle, is a fully autonomous, uncrewed cargo vessel designed to ferry up to 4.5 tons of cargo to and from the ISS. The spacecraft, manufactured by Mitsubishi Heavy Industries and operated by the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA), was launched from the Tanegashima Space Center on a H-IIB rocket. After a 10‑day flight, it performed a soft‑capture maneuver and secured itself to the station’s Harmony module, initiating a 30‑day docked period.

Engineering Highlights

HTV-X incorporates several firsts for Japan’s cargo fleet: a new solar‑panel array that boosts power generation by 20 %, an upgraded propulsion system using a more efficient liquid hydrogen/oxygen engine, and a cutting‑edge autonomous rendezvous suite that reduces pilot intervention to a minimum. The craft’s 12‑meter-long trunk houses a modular cargo bay with separate compartments for experiments, spare parts, and consumables, all protected by a hardened outer shell.

Launch and Docking

The launch sequence began at 08:00 JST, with the H‑IIB rocket lifting off at a 35.8‑degree inclination, targeting a 400‑km sun‑synchronous orbit. After a series of mid‑course corrections, HTV-X entered a phasing orbit that aligned its trajectory for the rendezvous window on Oct. 28. The spacecraft’s onboard computers performed a series of autonomous checks, culminating in a 2‑minute soft‑capture that secured the vehicle to the ISS’s docking port. Mission control logged the docking as “smooth” and “within margin.”

Live Viewing and Public Engagement

JAXA has streamed the entire docking sequence on its official YouTube channel, offering real‑time telemetry, camera feeds, and commentary from mission specialists. Viewers can watch the moment the HTV-X’s docking probe aligns with the ISS, the subsequent latch engagement, and the first exchange of cargo between the two vessels. The live feed also includes a short educational segment explaining the HTV-X’s systems and the importance of automated cargo deliveries for future lunar and Martian missions.
