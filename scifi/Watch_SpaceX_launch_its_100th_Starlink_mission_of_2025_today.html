Watch SpaceX launch its 100th Starlink mission of 2025 today

Summary: SpaceX will launch its 100th Starlink mission of the year today (Oct. 30), and you can watch the action live.

Launch Details

Today at 07:14 UTC, SpaceX will lift off from Cape Canaveral Space Force Station aboard a Falcon 9 rocket. The payload will comprise 60 new Starlink satellites, each equipped with the latest phased‑array antennas and inter‑satellite laser links that enable high‑throughput broadband coverage. The launch window is set for 10:14 local time (EDT), giving viewers a perfect opportunity to witness the ascent from the launch pad.

What to Expect

With over 1,500 satellites in orbit already, this 100th mission marks a milestone in SpaceX’s aggressive rollout schedule. The Falcon 9 will perform a controlled ascent, separating the first stage after a 2‑minute, 30‑second burn, and then deploying the Starlink buses into a 550‑kilometer orbit. The payload’s deployment sequence will be broadcast live, showcasing the precise spacing of each satellite as it reaches its designated slot in the constellation.

Live Stream Instructions

To catch every moment of the launch, head over to SpaceX’s official YouTube channel at youtube.com/user/spacexchannel. The live feed will start 30 minutes before liftoff, and you can also stream from the SpaceX app or the company’s dedicated launch countdown page. For a global audience, the event is also being streamed on the SpaceX Twitter account and the SpaceX website’s “Live” section.

Impact on the Starlink Network

Each new satellite enhances the network’s capacity and redundancy, enabling higher bandwidth per user and reducing latency across the globe. The 100th launch this year pushes the total constellation closer to 7,500 active satellites, which will support the projected 10‑million subscriber target by 2028. As the network densifies, users in remote and underserved regions will experience significantly improved connectivity, paving the way for applications ranging from broadband internet to real‑time IoT and autonomous vehicle communications.
