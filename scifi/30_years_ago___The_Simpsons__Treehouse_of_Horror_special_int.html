30 years ago, 'The Simpsons' Treehouse of Horror special introduced us to Kang & Kodos, the series' iconic alien invaders

Summary: Don't blame me, I voted for Kodos

From a Halloween Special to a Cultural Touchstone

The Treehouse of Horror III episode, which first aired on October 24, 1992, introduced the world to Kang and Kodos, the time‑traveling aliens who would become a recurring staple of The Simpsons. Their first appearance—a brief, menacing countdown—set the tone for the episode’s satirical take on extraterrestrial threats and the American obsession with political correctness.

Design and Voice: Crafting the Unforgettable Invaders

Artist and writer David Silverman conceived Kang’s towering, elongated form and Kodos’s slightly shorter, more comical silhouette. Voice actor Hank Azaria brought them to life with a distinct, high‑pitched timbre that has become instantly recognizable. The duo’s signature “I’m a little short, but I’m still a big threat” line captures their blend of menace and absurdity.

Political Satire and the “Kodos Vote” Phenomenon

In the episode’s climactic scene, Kodos delivers a chilling monologue about the dangers of voting for a “Kodos candidate.” The line resonated beyond television, becoming an online meme and a rallying cry for fans who felt the show’s satire mirrored real‑world political anxieties. It’s a testament to the writers’ skill in blending humor with pointed commentary.

Legacy: From Halloween Hallmarks to Pop Culture Icons

Since that first appearance, Kang and Kodos have appeared in almost every Treehouse of Horror episode, often parodying current events—from climate change to social media. Their recurring role underscores The Simpsons’ knack for using science‑fiction tropes to critique society, while the aliens themselves have spawned merchandise, fan art, and even a cameo in a 2014 feature film. Their enduring popularity proves that a simple, well‑executed concept can become a lasting cultural touchstone.
