Watch India's most powerful rocket launch a huge military communications satellite to orbit today

Summary: India is set to launch the CMS‑03 military communications satellite into orbit today (Nov. 2) from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre. The powerful PSLV‑C57 rocket will carry the 2,500‑kg satellite, boosting the country’s strategic communications network and demonstrating advanced launch capabilities.

Launch Overview

The PSLV‑C57, a proven workhorse in India’s space program, lifts off at 10:00 UTC from the First Launch Pad of the Satish Dhawan Space Centre. The launch window, spanning from 09:00 to 12:00 UTC, has been optimized to place CMS‑03 into a 500 km, 97‑degree sun‑synchronous orbit. Mission control will monitor the four‑stage ascent, with the third stage providing the final orbital insertion burn.

Mission Significance

CMS‑03 expands India’s secure military communications infrastructure, offering high‑bandwidth, low‑latency links for armed forces across the Indian Ocean region. The satellite will support command‑and‑control, video, and data transfer for naval, air, and ground units, enhancing operational readiness and resilience against cyber and electronic warfare threats.

Technical Details

The satellite houses an advanced phased‑array antenna system capable of multi‑beam operation, allowing simultaneous links with multiple ground stations. Powered by dual solar arrays, CMS‑03 will have a mission life of 15 years, with redundant propulsion and attitude‑control systems to ensure longevity and reliability.

Live Viewing

Watch the launch live on the Indian Space Research Organisation’s (ISRO) official YouTube channel and the ISRO Live portal. For a 360° view, stream the event through the dedicated ISRO mobile app, which offers real‑time telemetry overlays and commentary from launch experts.
