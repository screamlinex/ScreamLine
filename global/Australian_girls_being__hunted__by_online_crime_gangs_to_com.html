Global News / October 29, 2025

Australian girls being 'hunted' by online crime gangs to commit violent acts

Summary: Groups of young men are coercing and exploiting vulnerable Australian girls for 'fun', Australian police say. These online crime gangs target girls through social media, manipulating them into participating in violent acts or facilitating attacks on others.

The Rise of Online Crime Gangs

Since the onset of the pandemic, a surge in online radicalization and recruitment has been reported across the country. These groups, often organized through encrypted messaging apps, use targeted grooming techniques to lure minors into violent schemes.

How Victims Are Targeted

Victims typically begin as casual social media followers who receive flattery and false promises of friendship. Over weeks, perpetrators intensify the relationship, sharing incriminating content and demanding that the girls perform or document acts that could harm others.

Police have identified a pattern: the girls are first asked to record videos of themselves threatening or stalking others, then later coerced into more overt violent actions such as assault or property damage.

Law Enforcement Response

In response, the Australian Federal Police and state police forces have intensified joint operations to dismantle these networks. A new task force was established last month, employing data analytics to trace IP addresses and cross-reference online identities.

Authorities have also rolled out a national education campaign aimed at parents and educators, warning about the signs of grooming and encouraging early reporting.
