Global News / October 27, 2025

Hurricane Melissa could be strongest to ever hit Jamaica

Summary: Hurricane Melissa has been upgraded to a Category 5 storm, potentially becoming the strongest tropical cyclone ever to make landfall in Jamaica.

Meteorological Details

Melissa, which formed in the eastern Caribbean, has intensified rapidly over the past 24 hours. With sustained winds now topping 155 mph and a central pressure of 920 mb, the system meets the criteria for a Category 5 hurricane on the Saffir–Simpson scale. Forecast models predict a track that brings the eyewall directly over the island’s eastern coast within the next 48 hours.

Potential Impact on Jamaica

Should Melissa make landfall, the island could experience catastrophic wind damage, storm surge up to 12 feet, and widespread flooding. Coastal communities in St. Ann, Portland, and St. Thomas are at particular risk, as the storm’s eye could bring a sudden drop in pressure and a surge of rain exceeding 8 inches in 24 hours.

Preparedness Measures

The Jamaica Meteorological Service has issued a Level 4 alert, urging residents to secure property, stock emergency supplies, and evacuate low‑lying areas. The Ministry of Disaster Preparedness has activated emergency shelters and coordinated with the Red Cross to ensure medical and logistical support for affected communities.

Historical Context

Melissa’s projected intensity eclipses the record‑setting Hurricane Ivan (2004), which was the strongest storm on record to hit Jamaica with Category 4 winds. If the forecast track holds, Melissa would surpass Ivan’s 150 mph winds and become the most powerful hurricane ever to strike the island.
