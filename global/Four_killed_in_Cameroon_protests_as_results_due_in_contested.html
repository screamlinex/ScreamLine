Global News / October 27, 2025

Four killed in Cameroon protests as results due in contested election

Summary: In a tense pre‑election period, four protesters were killed during demonstrations in Cameroon, as the nation awaits the results of a contested presidential election that pits the 43‑year incumbent against a rising opposition challenger.

Violence and Casualties

Police and security forces opened fire on crowds in the capital, Douala, on Tuesday night, resulting in four confirmed deaths and dozens of injuries. The victims were identified as local activists who had gathered to demand a transparent vote. Authorities claim the clashes were sparked by a protest that turned violent, while opposition groups allege excessive force.

Election Context

President Paul Biya has ruled Cameroon since 1982, and his party has dominated the political landscape for decades. The opposition’s candidate, former opposition leader and former minister, has galvanized a broad coalition of civil society groups, promising reforms and a break from the status quo. The election, scheduled for next week, is seen as a turning point for the country’s democratic future.

International Reactions

The United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights issued a statement calling for an independent investigation into the killings. The African Union has urged both sides to engage in dialogue, while neighboring Nigeria expressed concern over potential spillover effects. Western governments have called for free and fair elections but have not yet imposed sanctions.
