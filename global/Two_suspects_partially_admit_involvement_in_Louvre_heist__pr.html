Global News / October 29, 2025

Two suspects partially admit involvement in Louvre heist, prosecutor says

Summary: The Paris prosecutor says the jewels are not yet recovered and there could be a bigger gang behind the theft.

Suspects' Confessions

The two men arrested after the Louvre heist have partially admitted to planning and executing the robbery, according to the prosecutor. They claimed to have coordinated the break‑in but denied taking the jewels themselves.

The Heist Unfolds

According to police reports, the suspects entered the museum at 2 a.m., bypassing the security system with a sophisticated lock‑picking device. They were seen in surveillance footage leaving the gallery with bags that later vanished.

Implications and Next Steps

The prosecutor warned that the missing jewels could be part of a larger, organized criminal network. Authorities are now expanding the investigation to trace any potential collaborators and track the jewels’ possible whereabouts.
