Global News / November 02, 2025

Dodgers seal back-to-back World Series titles

Summary: The Los Angeles Dodgers become the first team for 25 years to win back-to-back World Series after overcoming the Toronto Blue Jays in the deciding seventh game.

Game Highlights

The 7‑2 victory on Sunday was a showcase of clutch pitching and timely hitting. Dodgers starter Tyler Glasnow kept the Blue Jays at bay, allowing only one run in the first two innings and striking out five. In the bottom of the seventh, a single by Cody Bellinger sparked a rally that capped the Dodgers’ comeback.

Key Performances

Pitcher Freddie Freeman, who hit his 10th homer of the series, also contributed with a pivotal double in the sixth inning. Catcher Will Smith’s 3‑run RBI single and 4‑strikeout game added to a dominant offensive effort, while rookie shortstop Mookie Betts stole two bases and drove in three runs.

Historical Significance

With this win, the Dodgers become the first club since the 1998–1999 Chicago Cubs to secure consecutive World Series titles. Their triumph also marks the first time a team has finished the regular season with a .500 record yet gone on to win the championship, underscoring the unpredictability of baseball’s postseason.

Future Outlook

Looking ahead, the Dodgers will aim to build on this momentum as they prepare for a potential 2025 title run. The core of the team—Freeman, Bellinger, and Betts—remains healthy, and the club’s depth in pitching gives them confidence to face any challenge in the next season.
