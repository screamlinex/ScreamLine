Global News / October 27, 2025

US and China agree framework of trade deal ahead of Trump-Xi meeting

Summary: US Trade Secretary Katherine Tai announced that the United States and China will sign a trade framework ahead of the upcoming Trump‑Xi meeting, with TikTok, rare earths, and tariff adjustments identified as key components of the agreement.

Framework Highlights

The deal, which will be signed during the leaders' summit, focuses on three pillars: the fate of TikTok, the export of rare earth minerals, and the structure of tariff reductions. Tai confirmed that the U.S. will lift its ban on TikTok’s Chinese parent, ByteDance, provided the platform complies with U.S. data‑security standards and adopts a transparent governance model.

China, in turn, has agreed to open its rare‑earth market to U.S. companies, subject to a phased approach that respects its domestic strategic interests. Both sides will also negotiate a tariff framework that balances U.S. agricultural interests with China’s push for lower import duties on key consumer goods.

Implications for the Tech Sector

Tech firms across the globe are watching closely. If TikTok’s U.S. operations are secured, it could set a precedent for how foreign tech companies navigate U.S. regulatory scrutiny. Meanwhile, the rare‑earth clause may ease supply chain pressures for American manufacturers of electric vehicles and high‑tech devices.

However, analysts warn that the agreement’s success hinges on detailed enforcement mechanisms, especially regarding data‑privacy protocols and the timeline for tariff reductions.

Next Steps

Following the framework signing, the U.S. and China will convene a joint committee to draft the final trade agreement. The committee will tackle implementation timelines, dispute‑resolution procedures, and compliance monitoring.

Both administrations have indicated that the Trump‑Xi meeting will serve as a platform to solidify these commitments and address any remaining concerns, potentially setting the stage for a broader U.S.–China economic partnership.
