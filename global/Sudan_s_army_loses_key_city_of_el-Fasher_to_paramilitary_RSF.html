Global News / October 28, 2025

Sudan's army loses key city of el-Fasher to paramilitary RSF after 18-month siege

Summary: The United Nations has issued an urgent statement condemning alleged atrocities committed by the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) in the newly captured city of el‑Fasher, calling for an independent investigation and immediate cessation of violence.

Fall of el‑Fasher

After an 18‑month siege, the RSF broke through the Sudanese army’s defenses and seized the key city of el‑Fasher, a pivotal moment that shifted the balance of power in the western region.

RSF's Alleged Atrocities

UN investigators have reported a pattern of systematic abuses, including looting, extrajudicial killings, and targeted attacks on civilian populations. Witness accounts and satellite imagery suggest widespread violations of international humanitarian law.

International Reactions

In a rare unified statement, the UN Security Council demanded an immediate halt to hostilities and the establishment of a neutral inquiry. Several governments and humanitarian NGOs have called for accountability and the protection of civilians.

Humanitarian Crisis

With el‑Fasher now under RSF control, thousands of displaced residents face acute shortages of food, clean water, and medical supplies. Aid agencies warn that the situation could deteriorate into a full‑blown humanitarian emergency if no relief reaches the city.

Future Outlook

Security analysts predict continued clashes as the RSF consolidates its hold, while diplomatic efforts intensify to broker a ceasefire. The international community remains on high alert, monitoring the unfolding crisis closely.
