Global News / October 24, 2025

Ontario to stop running anti-tariff advert that angered Trump

Summary: Ontario will pause its controversial anti‑tariff advertisement that drew a sharp rebuke from President Trump, allowing trade talks with the U.S. to resume. Premier Doug Ford announced that the ad will be taken off the air from Monday, citing the need to smooth diplomatic tensions and advance Ontario’s trade agenda.

What Went Wrong?

The ad, which featured a bold slogan warning U.S. manufacturers about potential tariffs, was broadcast on Ontario’s provincial television network. Its provocative imagery and direct critique of U.S. trade policy triggered an immediate backlash from the Trump administration, which saw the message as an affront to American sovereignty. The timing—just days before a scheduled trade summit—only amplified the diplomatic fallout.

Reaction from the U.S.

White House officials issued a formal statement demanding a swift apology and calling the advertisement “unacceptable.” President Trump’s spokesperson described the ad as “disrespectful” and warned that it could derail ongoing negotiations. Meanwhile, U.S. trade representatives urged Canada to adopt a more collaborative tone in future communications.

Ontario’s Trade Strategy

Premier Doug Ford emphasized that the pause is a strategic move to keep trade talks on track. “Ontario’s priority is to secure favorable terms for our manufacturers and protect jobs,” he said. The province has been lobbying for lower tariff rates on automotive parts, a sector that accounts for a significant portion of its export economy.

What’s Next for the Advert?

While the ad will be off the air, the government plans to revisit its messaging strategy. Analysts suggest that a more nuanced, data‑driven approach could better resonate with U.S. stakeholders. In the meantime, trade officials are preparing a new campaign that highlights Ontario’s role as a reliable partner rather than a threat.
