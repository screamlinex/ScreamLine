Global News / October 28, 2025

BBC 'deeply concerned' about journalist unable to leave Vietnam

Summary: The BBC has expressed deep concern after a senior journalist has remained in Vietnam for several months, unable to depart due to the authorities withholding a renewed passport. The situation highlights growing tensions between the media and the Vietnamese government, raising questions about press freedom and diplomatic resolution efforts.

Background of the Case

The journalist, who has worked with the BBC for over a decade, was in Vietnam for an investigative series on Southeast Asian trade practices. Their passport was set to expire in late 2023, and the BBC applied for a renewal through the embassy. Despite repeated requests, the Vietnamese authorities have yet to return the updated passport, leaving the reporter stranded on Vietnamese soil.

BBC’s Response and Call for Action

BBC Director of Global News, Dr. Elena Kwon, issued a statement urging the Vietnamese government to "respect international journalistic standards and to ensure the safe exit of their staff." The BBC has also appealed to the UK Foreign Office to intervene diplomatically, citing the importance of safeguarding its employees abroad.

Vietnamese Government’s Position

Officials from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs have stated that the passport renewal process is "currently under review" due to "administrative delays." They have denied any political motivation, emphasizing that they remain committed to maintaining "transparent and fair procedures" for foreign nationals.

International Reaction and Press Freedom Implications

The Committee to Protect Journalists and Reporters Without Borders have both condemned the delay, labeling it a "potential violation of press freedom." Human rights organizations are urging for a swift resolution, noting that such incidents could deter foreign media from covering sensitive topics in Vietnam.
