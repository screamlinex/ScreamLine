Global News / October 28, 2025

Man pleads guilty to killing Japan's former PM Shinzo Abe

Summary: Tetsuya Yamagami, 45, shot former Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe multiple times at a 2022 political rally, leading to a court case that concluded with Yamagami pleading guilty and facing a life sentence.

Background

Shinzo Abe, Japan’s longest-serving prime minister, was assassinated on July 8, 2022, during a campaign event in Nara Prefecture. Yamagami, a 45‑year‑old former marine, had a history of extremist beliefs and had been monitored by security agencies for years.

Court Proceedings

The trial began in September 2023, with Yamagami confessing to the shooting, citing political motives and personal grievances. He pleaded guilty on December 15, 2024, after prosecutors offered a plea deal that included a life sentence without parole.

Public Reaction

Japanese officials condemned the attack, with the government announcing enhanced security measures for public officials. Public opinion polls show a surge in support for tighter gun control and increased funding for mental health services.

Implications for Japanese Politics

The case has intensified discussions about political safety protocols and the role of extremist ideology in Japan. Analysts warn that Abe’s death may shift the balance of power within the Liberal Democratic Party and accelerate reforms on political accountability.
