Global News / October 29, 2025

Cameroon opposition leader to face legal action over election unrest, government says

Summary: The threat against Issa Tchiroma Bakary comes a day after he was declared the runner‑up in disputed presidential polls, prompting the government to warn that legal action will follow his alleged involvement in the unrest that erupted after the election results were announced.

Background

Issa Tchiroma Bakary, the charismatic leader of the Union of the Peoples of Cameroon (UPC), has long been a vocal critic of President Paul Biya’s administration. The recent presidential election, held on 27 February, was marred by accusations of irregularities, leading to protests in major cities. Tchiroma, who secured an estimated 30% of the vote, was officially placed second behind Biya, who claimed a 54% victory.

Government Statement

In a statement released by the Ministry of Justice, officials warned that Tchiroma would face “serious legal proceedings” for allegedly “inciting violence” during the post‑election demonstrations. The statement also cited the UPC’s failure to comply with court orders to restrain supporters, calling the party’s actions “disruptive and unlawful.”

Tchiroma’s Response

Speaking from his Lagos headquarters, Tchiroma denied any intent to foment violence, calling the government’s threats “politically motivated intimidation.” He urged his supporters to remain peaceful and reminded the public that “the only way to win the struggle for democracy is through dialogue, not bloodshed.” The UPC’s spokesperson also announced plans to file a complaint with the African Union’s human rights commission.

International Reactions

The United Nations and several European Parliament members have expressed concern over the escalating tensions, urging both sides to engage in constructive dialogue. Human rights NGOs, including Amnesty International, have called for an independent investigation into the alleged abuses by security forces during the protests.

Implications for Cameroon's Political Landscape

With the election results still contested, the looming legal action against Tchiroma could further polarize an already divided nation. Analysts predict that the move may either silence the opposition’s leadership or galvanize supporters into a more organized, albeit potentially more volatile, opposition movement. As the political climate heats up, observers will watch closely for any shifts in the government’s approach to dissent.
