Global News / October 26, 2025

Egypt and Red Cross join search for hostage bodies in Gaza

Summary: Egypt and the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) have joined forces to search for the bodies of hostages in Gaza, following a recent Israeli decision to allow teams to operate beyond the so‑called “yellow line” within Israeli‑controlled territory.

Egypt's Diplomatic Push

The Egyptian Foreign Ministry announced that Cairo will deploy a senior delegation to Gaza to negotiate with Israeli authorities and ensure the safe passage of humanitarian teams. Egyptian President Abdel Fattah el‑Sisi has urged the international community to intensify pressure on Israel to honor the “yellow line” restrictions while allowing full access to the areas where hostages were last seen.

Red Cross' Humanitarian Mission

The ICRC has mobilized a specialized search and rescue unit comprising forensic experts, medics, and logistics personnel. The team will work closely with Egyptian officials and Israeli military commanders to locate and identify the remains of hostages, with a priority on providing closure for families and preventing further trauma.

Israeli Coordination

In a statement released by the Israeli Defense Forces (IDF), commanders confirmed that the “yellow line” has been officially extended to include additional checkpoints and residential zones. The IDF will provide secure corridors, surveillance support, and intelligence sharing to facilitate the joint search operations.

Local Reactions

Residents in the Gaza Strip have expressed cautious optimism, hoping the expanded search will bring answers. Human rights groups, however, emphasize that the presence of armed Israeli forces may still pose risks to the safety of humanitarian workers, calling for strict adherence to international humanitarian law.
