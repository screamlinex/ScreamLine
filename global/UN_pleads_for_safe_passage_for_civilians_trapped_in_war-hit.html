Global News / October 27, 2025

UN pleads for safe passage for civilians trapped in war-hit Sudan city

Summary: The fall of el‑Fasher would cement the RSF's control of the west and reinforce a de facto split in the nation.

UN Urges Immediate Safe Passage

The United Nations has called for an immediate ceasefire and the establishment of a humanitarian corridor to allow civilians trapped in the besieged city of el‑Fasher to escape the violence. In a statement released Monday, the UN Secretary‑General emphasized that “no civilian should be left behind” and urged all parties to respect international humanitarian law.

El‑Fasher: A City on the Brink

Located in West Kordofan, el‑Fasher has become the focal point of a brutal clash between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF). Over the past weeks, the city has seen repeated shelling, air strikes and ground assaults, leaving its infrastructure in tatters and its residents in a state of emergency.

RSF Consolidates Western Sudan

With the RSF's recent gains, the western region of Sudan is effectively split into two spheres of influence. The RSF’s control over el‑Fasher would not only secure a strategic logistical hub but also solidify its dominance over the entire west, deepening the country’s de facto political divide.

International Community Responds

Western governments, the African Union and regional partners have urged restraint, but concrete action remains limited. While the UN has pressed for a ceasefire, sanctions and diplomatic pressure have yet to translate into a tangible shift on the ground.

Civilians Speak Out

Families in el‑Fasher are living in constant fear, with many reporting shortages of food, water and medical supplies. “We have no safe place to go,” says one mother, highlighting the urgent need for international intervention to protect those caught in the crossfire.
