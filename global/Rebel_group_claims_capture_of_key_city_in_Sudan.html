Global News / October 26, 2025

Rebel group claims capture of key city in Sudan

Summary: The RSF’s capture of el‑Fasher marks a pivotal moment, solidifying its dominance in western Sudan and deepening the nation’s split. The city, a critical logistical hub, now falls under rebel control, signalling a shift in regional power dynamics and raising urgent questions about the future of Sudan’s fragile ceasefire.

RSF's Tactical Masterstroke

In a swift, coordinated assault that began at dawn, RSF forces overwhelmed the government garrison in el‑Fasher. With air support and a barrage of artillery, the rebels breached the city’s outer defenses, forcing a rapid withdrawal of loyalist troops. The operation, reportedly executed in under six hours, showcased the RSF’s growing operational sophistication and its ability to seize urban centers with precision.

Strategic Importance of el‑Fasher

El‑Fasher sits on a major highway linking Sudan’s capital to the western border, making it a logistical linchpin for both supply chains and troop movements. Control of the city grants the RSF a forward base from which to launch further offensives into the west, potentially cutting off government forces from critical resources and tightening the rebels’ grip on the region.

Local Impact and Civilian Stories

For the residents of el‑Fasher, the takeover has been a whirlwind of uncertainty. “We were told the city would be safe, but the streets are now filled with armed men,” says local shopkeeper Amina Ahmed. While some civilians have fled, others have stayed, hoping the RSF’s presence will bring stability after months of conflict. Humanitarian agencies warn that the influx of displaced families could strain the city’s limited infrastructure.

Global Reactions and Diplomatic Tides

International actors have responded with caution. The United Nations has called for an immediate ceasefire in the region, while the African Union urges both sides to engage in dialogue. Meanwhile, regional powers, including Egypt and Ethiopia, monitor the shift closely, wary of how the new power balance might affect cross-border security and trade.

The Road Ahead: A Nation Divided

With el‑Fasher now under RSF control, the western front of Sudan’s civil war has become more defined. Analysts warn that the city’s fall could accelerate the fragmentation of the country, making a unified political solution increasingly elusive. As the RSF consolidates its gains, the international community faces a critical decision: how to respond to a new reality on the ground while still championing peace and human rights.
