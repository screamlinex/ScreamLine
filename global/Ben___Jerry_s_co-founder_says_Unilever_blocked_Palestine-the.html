Global News / October 29, 2025

Ben & Jerry's co-founder says Unilever blocked Palestine-themed ice cream

Summary: Ben Cohen, co‑founder of Ben & Jerry’s, has announced that Unilever’s parent company will not allow a Palestine‑themed ice cream, prompting him to launch a new, independent watermelon flavor as a stand‑in for “permanent peace in Palestine.”

Unilever’s Position on Themed Flavors

In a brief statement, Unilever clarified that its brand guidelines prohibit the use of political or geographic themes in product names. The company reiterated its focus on “neutral, universally appealing” product lines to maintain global market harmony.

Cohen’s Bold Counter‑Move

During a livestreamed Q&A, Ben Cohen expressed disappointment at Unilever’s stance, saying the company “seems to be silencing a message that matters.” He vowed to “take matters into his own hands” by creating a fresh, independent flavor—watermelon—to symbolize hope and unity in Palestine.

Watermelon: A Symbolic Sweetener

Watermelon, with its bright red seeds and lush green rind, has long been a symbol of peace and renewal. Cohen explained that the new flavor will feature real watermelon pieces, a splash of citrus, and a hint of mint to create a “taste of serenity.” The product will be sold exclusively in select U.S. markets for the first six months.

Community and Consumer Response

Early reactions from fans have been enthusiastic, with social media accounts flooding the hashtag #PeaceInPalaceWatermelon. Some retailers have already pledged to carry the flavor, citing “support for creative freedom and social justice.” Meanwhile, critics argue that the move risks commercializing a political cause.

What’s Next for Ben & Jerry’s?

Cohen said he will continue to push for “dialogue through dessert” and hinted at future collaborations with grassroots organizations. “It’s about using what we love to spark conversation and bring people together,” he said, adding that the watermelon flavor is just the beginning of a broader, “taste‑based peace initiative.”
