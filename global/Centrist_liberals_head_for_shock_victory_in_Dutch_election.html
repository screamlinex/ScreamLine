Global News / October 29, 2025

Centrist liberals head for shock victory in Dutch election, exit poll says

Summary: Rob Jetten's D66 party celebrates, two years after his party languished in fifth place in the last vote.

Exit Poll Shock

Exit polls released this morning sent a ripple through the Dutch political landscape, indicating that D66, led by Rob Jetten, will secure the largest share of the vote, a stunning reversal from their fifth-place finish two years ago. Analysts suggest that a surge in centrist sentiment, combined with effective campaigning on climate and digital innovation, has tipped the balance in D66’s favor.

Rob Jetten's Response

Jetten, who has long been the face of D66's progressive agenda, greeted the results with a mix of gratitude and resolve. In a televised statement, he thanked the party's volunteers and pledged to keep the focus on “pragmatic solutions that unite rather than divide.” He also hinted at a willingness to negotiate with both left-leaning and moderate parties to form a stable coalition.

Coalition Outlook

With 30% of the vote projected, D66 now sits on the front lines of coalition talks. Early discussions point to a possible partnership with the Social Democrats (PvdA) and the green party GroenLinks, creating a centrist‑green bloc that could command a decisive majority. However, the rise of the far‑right Party for Freedom (PVV) still looms, and negotiations will have to navigate complex policy trade‑offs.

Broader Impact

The shock victory signals a shift in Dutch politics, where voters appear increasingly disillusioned with traditional parties. Experts say that D66’s success could catalyze a broader move toward centrist, issue‑driven politics, potentially reshaping the country’s policy agenda on climate action, digital infrastructure, and social welfare. International observers are watching closely, as the outcome may influence European policy coordination and the EU’s stance on migration and economic recovery.
