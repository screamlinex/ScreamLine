Trump and Xi held face-to-face trade talks – what did they agree?

Summary: In a landmark summit held in Seoul, President Donald Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping met face‑to‑face to address escalating trade tensions. The two leaders reached a preliminary agreement to reduce tariffs on key goods, streamline customs procedures, and enhance cooperation on technology standards, setting the stage for a more balanced and predictable trade relationship between the two largest economies.

Meeting Context

The talks were convened after weeks of heightened economic friction, including the U.S. imposition of steep tariffs on Chinese steel and aluminum, and China’s retaliatory measures on U.S. agricultural exports. Both administrations cited the need for stability in global supply chains and the risks of a protracted trade war.

Key Agreements

1. Tariff Reduction: Both sides agreed to lower tariffs on a list of 200 products, including U.S. soybeans and Chinese smartphones, over a phased schedule.

2. Customs Streamlining: A joint task force will work on reducing inspection times and simplifying documentation for cross‑border shipments.

3. Technology Standards: The leaders committed to aligning certain standards in telecommunications and semiconductor manufacturing to reduce trade barriers.

Implications for US‑China Trade

The accord is expected to lift some pressure on American farmers and manufacturers while granting Chinese firms easier access to U.S. markets. Analysts warn that the agreement is still fragile, with enforcement mechanisms yet to be fully defined.

Future Outlook

Both presidents signaled a willingness to revisit trade issues in the coming months, with a follow‑up meeting slated for early 2025. The success of the Seoul talks will largely hinge on the continued cooperation of lower‑level officials and the ability of each side to translate the preliminary consensus into concrete policy changes.
