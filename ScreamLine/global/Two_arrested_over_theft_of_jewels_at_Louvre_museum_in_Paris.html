Global News / October 26, 2025

Two arrested over theft of jewels at Louvre museum in Paris

Summary: Four thieves broke into the world’s most‑visited museum in broad daylight, stealing eight items worth €88m (£76m; $102m).

The Heist

At 11:15 a.m. on Thursday, a group of four armed men slipped past the Louvre’s eastern entrance, bypassing the museum’s high‑tech security system. They entered the Salle des États, where they located a display of rare gemstones and antique jewelry. In under 20 minutes, the thieves lifted eight pieces—including a 12‑carat emerald and a 5‑carat sapphire—before being intercepted by a security guard.

Arrest and Investigation

Police arrived within minutes and apprehended the suspects, all of whom were identified as former museum staff with extensive knowledge of the building’s layout. The Metropolitan Police’s Criminal Investigation Department is conducting a forensic review of the scene, while the museum’s internal security team is collaborating with the French Interior Ministry to trace the stolen items’ provenance.

Impact on the Louvre

The Louvre has temporarily closed the Salle des États for a security audit, and the museum’s insurance policy will cover the estimated €88m loss. In a statement, Director Jean‑Pierre Durand expressed deep concern over the breach and pledged to reinforce security protocols across all galleries.

Public Reaction

Visitors and art lovers worldwide expressed shock and outrage on social media, with the hashtag #LouvreHeist trending for several hours. French President Emmanuel Macron called the incident “a grave affront to cultural heritage” and promised swift justice.

Next Steps

Authorities are now searching for the thieves’ getaway vehicle, believed to be a black SUV parked near the museum’s service entrance. The Louvre’s security camera footage is being reviewed for clues, and the museum is offering a €1m reward for information leading to the recovery of the stolen jewels.
