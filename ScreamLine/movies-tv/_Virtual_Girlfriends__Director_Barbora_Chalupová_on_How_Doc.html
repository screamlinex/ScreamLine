‘Virtual Girlfriends’ Director Barbora Chalupová on How Doc Is About Loneliness, Not Sex, and Her Upcoming Fiction Debut (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: Barbora Chalupová, the visionary behind the OnlyFans documentary “Virtual Girlfriends,” is set to channel her intimate exploration of digital intimacy into a fiction film titled “On Tranquility of Mind.” Drawing inspiration from the enigmatic Madame Cobra, a character rooted in S&M culture, Chalupová aims to shift the narrative from sensational sex to the profound loneliness that pervades online relationships.

The Doc’s True Focus

While “Virtual Girlfriends” initially drew attention for its candid look at sex work on OnlyFans, Chalupová insists the core of the documentary is the emotional isolation that many creators experience behind the screens. “I wanted to humanize the people behind the avatars,” she explains, noting how the film’s intimate interviews reveal a yearning for genuine connection that often goes unnoticed in mainstream portrayals.

From Reality to Fiction

In her upcoming feature, Chalupová will transpose these real‑world themes into a stylized narrative. “On Tranquility of Mind” will follow a protagonist navigating a cyber‑cultured world while grappling with her own sense of self‑worth. By blending documentary sensibilities with cinematic storytelling, she hopes to create a “mirror” that reflects the same loneliness that defined the doc.

Madame Cobra: The Spark

The character of Madame Cobra—an S&M practitioner who offers both guidance and caution—serves as the linchpin of Chalupová’s new project. “She’s more than a fetish icon; she’s a repository of stories about power, vulnerability, and the human need for validation,” Chalupová says. “Her backstory is a testament to how we negotiate identity in a world that commodifies intimacy.”

Looking Ahead

Beyond “On Tranquility of Mind,” Chalupová is already sketching ideas for a series that could delve deeper into the intersection of technology, sexuality, and mental health. “The conversation is just getting started,” she notes. “I’m excited to keep pushing the boundaries of how we talk about loneliness in the digital age.”
