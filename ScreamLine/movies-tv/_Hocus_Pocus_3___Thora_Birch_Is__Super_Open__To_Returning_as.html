‘Hocus Pocus 3’: Thora Birch Is ‘Super Open’ To Returning as Dani Dennison

Summary: Disney+ is moving forward with a third installment of the beloved Halloween classic “Hocus Pocus,” and lead actress Thora Birch has expressed a strong willingness to reprise her role as young Dani Dennison. In an interview with Entertainment Tonight, Birch said she’s “super open” to returning, noting she’s curious about the creative direction the writers will take in reintroducing Dani. The studio’s plans for the new film, fan buzz, and potential storyline twists are all on the table as production moves into pre‑production.

Thora Birch’s Enthusiasm for a Return

During a sit‑down with Entertainment Tonight, Birch shared that she’s excited about the possibility of coming back as the spirited teenage witch who first stole the show in 1993. “I’d love to see how they land on bringing Dani back,” she said, adding that she’s “super open” to whatever narrative path the writers choose. Birch’s comments come as the Disney+ team confirms the project’s development, and her willingness could help shape the film’s tone and continuity.

Disney+ Sets the Stage for a New Halloween Chapter

Disney+ has officially green‑lit “Hocus Pocus 3,” with a projected release date in the 2025–2026 window. The studio is reportedly assembling a fresh creative team that will honor the original’s charm while aiming to attract a new generation of viewers. Production is slated to begin in late 2024, with a mix of returning cast members and new faces expected to appear on screen.

Potential Storyline for Dani Dennison

While specific plot details remain under wraps, early leaks suggest a storyline that could explore the aftermath of the 1993 Salem witch trials from a modern perspective. Dani’s return may involve her discovering a new spell or confronting the legacy of her family’s magical mishaps. Birch hinted that she’s curious about how the script will balance nostalgia with contemporary themes, potentially offering a fresh take on the witchy lore.

Fan Reactions and Cultural Impact

Social media has already erupted with speculation, with fans expressing excitement over the possibility of seeing Thora Birch back in her iconic role. The original film’s cult status continues to thrive, especially during Halloween season, and many hope that the new installment will preserve the humor, heart, and supernatural flair that made the first two movies classics. Disney+ is likely to capitalize on this enthusiasm with targeted marketing and behind‑the‑scenes content.
