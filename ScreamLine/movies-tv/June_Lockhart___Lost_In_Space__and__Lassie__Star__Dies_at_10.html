June Lockhart, ‘Lost In Space’ and ‘Lassie’ Star, Dies at 100

Summary: June Lockhart, a beloved actress of the 1950s and ’60s known for her roles in “Lost In Space,” “Lassie,” and “Meet Me in St. Louis,” died of natural causes on Oct. 23 in Santa Monica, Calif. She was 100. Lockhart became known for her performances as Timmy’s foster mother, Ruth Martin, on the CBS series “Lost In Space.”

Early Life and Breakthrough

Born in 1925 in San Antonio, Texas, Lockhart began performing as a child in theater and radio, eventually moving to Hollywood where she signed with Paramount in the early 1940s. Her first film roles came in the late 1940s, but it was her television work that would bring her national fame.
