Lucía Aleñar Iglesias’ Award-Winning ‘Forastera’ Acquired for U.S. by Grasshopper Film, Lands Spanish, Japanese and Swedish Distribution (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: Fresh off winning the Fipresci Prize for Emerging Filmmakers at Toronto, Lucía Aleñar Iglesias’ “Forastera” has secured U.S. distribution with Grasshopper Film. The Spanish drama also landed deals in Spain (Atalante), Sweden (Folkets Bio), and Japan (Starcat), positioning it for a global festival run and theatrical release across key markets.

U.S. Deal with Grasshopper Film

Grasshopper Film, known for championing independent cinema, has signed “Forastera” for North American theatrical and streaming distribution. The partnership aims to introduce Iglesias’s debut feature to U.S. audiences through a multi‑platform release, leveraging Grasshopper’s strong festival‑centric marketing expertise.

International Distribution Partners

In Spain, Madrid‑based Atalante will handle domestic theatrical distribution, ensuring the film reaches both mainstream and art‑house audiences. Sweden’s Folkets Bio, a distributor celebrated for curating socially conscious cinema, will bring “Forastera” to Swedish audiences. In Japan, Starcat will oversee theatrical and digital releases, tapping into the country’s growing appetite for nuanced foreign dramas.

Festival Momentum & Awards

After its triumphant debut at the Toronto International Film Festival—where it claimed the Fipresci Prize for Emerging Filmmakers—“Forastera” has already generated buzz at Venice, Locarno, and Rotterdam. The film’s critical acclaim has accelerated its acquisition pipeline, with distributors eager to capitalize on its festival accolades.

Director’s Vision & Future Outlook

Lucía Aleñar Iglesias reflects on the film’s journey: “Winning the Fipresci award validated the risk we took to tell this intimate story. With these distribution partners, we hope to spark conversations about belonging and identity across cultures.” The team anticipates a staggered release, beginning with a limited U.S. run in late 2025, followed by international premieres in early 2026.
