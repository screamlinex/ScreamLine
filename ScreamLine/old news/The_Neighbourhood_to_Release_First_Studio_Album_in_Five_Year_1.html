The Neighbourhood to Release First Studio Album in Five Years

Summary: The reunited rock band The Neighbourhood announced their first studio album in five years, releasing three new tracks—“Private,” “OMG,” and “Lovebomb”—as a preview.

Album Announcement

The Neighbourhood confirmed that their upcoming album, titled Neon Horizons, will be released on October 18, 2025. This marks the band's first full-length project since 2020.

Lead vocalist Jesse Rutherford stated that the album reflects the band's evolution and the personal growth experienced during the hiatus.

New Tracks Preview

Three new songs were released ahead of the album launch. “Private” opens with atmospheric synth layers and introspective lyrics, while “OMG” delivers a punchy, guitar-driven hook.

“Lovebomb” showcases a blend of melodic pop and edgy rock, featuring a soaring chorus that the band described as their most ambitious composition to date.

Band Reunion

The lineup for the new material includes original members Jesse Rutherford, Zachary "Zac" Vandenberg, and drummer Ryan McKay, alongside touring guitarist Tyler Johnson.

After a period of individual projects, the group reconvened in early 2024 to begin writing and recording the album.

Release Details

“Neon Horizons” will be available on all major streaming platforms and in physical formats, including CD and vinyl. The record is produced by longtime collaborator John Green and will be distributed by RCA Records.

Pre-orders for the album began on August 1, offering exclusive bonus tracks for fans who purchase through the official website.
