Steven Soderbergh Says He ‘Did Not Enjoy’ Hiding ‘The Hunt for Ben Solo’ From Fans: ‘It Really Did Need to Remain a Secret’

Summary: Steven Soderbergh admitted he didn’t enjoy keeping the unmade Star Wars film “The Hunt for Ben Solo” a secret, but maintained that the secrecy was essential for protecting the project’s creative vision and the rights involved.

The Backstory

Adam Driver’s recent interview with the Associated Press brought the long‑concealed project into the spotlight. The film, which was slated to follow the fate of Kylo Ren’s brother, was never officially greenlit by Lucasfilm. Soderbergh, who had been attached as director, had been working on a treatment and a script but never saw the project through to production.

Soderbergh’s Perspective

In a candid BlueSky post, the director confessed that he “did not enjoy” hiding the project from fans. He explained that the secrecy was not about ego but about safeguarding the creative process and the legal complexities of the Star Wars franchise. “It really did need to remain a secret,” he wrote, adding that revealing too early could have jeopardized the story’s integrity.

Fan Reactions

The revelation sparked a flurry of speculation online. Some fans praised Soderbergh for his honesty, while others expressed disappointment that a potential Star Wars spin‑off never materialized. Twitter threads debated whether the secrecy was a necessary protective measure or an unnecessary cloak that fueled rumors.

What Comes Next?

While Soderbergh has not ruled out revisiting the concept, he remains focused on his current projects. The film’s status remains uncertain, and Lucasfilm has not announced any plans to revive the storyline. For now, the hunt for Ben Solo remains a “what if” scenario for Star Wars enthusiasts.
