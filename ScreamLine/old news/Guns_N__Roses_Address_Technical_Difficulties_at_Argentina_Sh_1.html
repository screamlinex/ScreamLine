Guns N’ Roses Address Technical Difficulties at Argentina Show

Summary: Guns N’ Roses performed in Buenos Aires on Thursday, but the show was marred by a series of technical problems, including faulty sound equipment, a malfunctioning stage rig, and a brief power outage. The band addressed the issues on stage, apologized to the audience, and finished the set with a strong encore.

Show Overview

The concert took place at the Estadio Obras Sanitarias, with an attendance of roughly 15,000 fans. The setlist included classic hits such as “Sweet Child o’ Mine,” “November Rain,” and a new track from the band’s upcoming album.

Technical Difficulties

Midway through the second set, the soundboard failed to transmit the guitar feed, causing a noticeable drop in volume. Shortly after, the lighting rig stalled, leaving the stage in dimmed conditions for several minutes. A brief power outage further delayed the performance, prompting the venue’s crew to restore electricity.

Band’s Response

Lead vocalist Axl Rose stepped onto the stage and acknowledged the problems, issuing a brief apology to the crowd. He assured fans that the crew was working to resolve the issues and that the band would finish the show as planned. The band’s guitarist, Slash, remained on stage, playing through the technical glitches to keep the momentum.

Fan Reaction

Despite the setbacks, audience members remained enthusiastic, cheering when the band returned to full power. Social media posts from attendees highlighted the band’s professionalism and the crowd’s supportive attitude.

Future Plans

Guns N’ Roses confirmed that the technical issues were isolated and that no additional shows in the region are affected. The band will resume touring next month in São Paulo, where the crew has implemented extra safety checks to prevent similar incidents.
