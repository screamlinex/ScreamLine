Miley Cyrus Channeled Past Experience With ‘Fire and Ash’ Into Original Song for ‘Avatar’

Summary: Miley Cyrus has released an original track, “Fire and Ash,” for the “Avatar” soundtrack, describing the project as profoundly meaningful. The song, co‑written and produced with Mark Ronson and Andrew Wyatt, draws on Cyrus’s personal experiences and debuted on the official soundtrack on October 15, 2025, where it entered the Billboard Hot 100.

Collaboration with Ronson and Wyatt

Miley Cyrus teamed up with producer Mark Ronson and songwriter Andrew Wyatt to craft “Fire and Ash.” Ronson noted the trio’s goal was to blend Cyrus’s emotive vocal style with contemporary pop production, while Wyatt contributed lyrical depth rooted in the “Avatar” narrative.

Inspiration from Past Experience

In a statement to the press, Cyrus said, “This project holds profound meaning to me.” She explained that the song reflects her journey of healing after the public scrutiny she faced during the “Fire and Ash” tour, channeling that resilience into the track’s themes of transformation and empowerment.

Song Details and Release

“Fire and Ash” runs 3:28 and features a soaring chorus that echoes the film’s motifs of fire and rebirth. The track was released digitally on all major streaming platforms as part of the “Avatar” soundtrack on October 15, 2025, and was accompanied by a lyric video directed by Hannah Carter.

Critical Reception

Early reviews praised the song’s blend of pop sensibility and cinematic atmosphere. Music critic Laura Kim of Rolling Stone called it “a powerful anthem that bridges pop and the epic tone of the franchise.” Billboard reported the track debuted at number 58 on the Hot 100, marking Cyrus’s 15th top‑100 entry.
