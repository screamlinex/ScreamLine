Watch Poppy Join Linkin Park for Classic Song During Tour Kickoff

Summary: YouTube: Daniel Calderon / Matt Winkelmeyer, Getty Images for The Recording Academy / Canva

Event Overview

During the opening night of Linkin Park’s “A Decade of Rock” tour, the band invited pop‑rock artist Poppy to join them onstage in Los Angeles. The surprise collaboration was captured by YouTube’s Daniel Calderon and Getty Images photographer Matt Winkelmeyer, and was later shared by the Recording Academy and Canva for promotional use.

Performance Details

Poppy appeared during the performance of the band’s 2000 hit “In the End.” She sang the chorus alongside vocalist Mike Shinoda and guitarist Brad Delson, while the full band played the original arrangement. The segment lasted roughly three minutes and was followed by a brief backstage interview with Poppy discussing her admiration for Linkin Park’s influence on her own music.

Reactions and Significance

Fans praised the unexpected duet, noting the contrast between Poppy’s experimental style and Linkin Park’s nu‑metal roots. Social media posts highlighted the moment as a testament to the band’s willingness to blend genres and support emerging artists. Industry analysts view the collaboration as a strategic move to broaden the tour’s appeal to younger audiences.
