Every 'Star Wars: Visions Volume 3' episode, ranked from worst to best

Summary: In the third installment of the award‑winning “Star Wars Visions,” five Japanese studios re‑imagined iconic moments from the franchise, each bringing a distinct artistic voice and cutting‑edge animation technology to the table. Ranking the shorts from the least to the most compelling, we’ll explore how each team balanced visual flair with narrative depth, and what technical innovations—ranging from real‑time rendering pipelines to hybrid hand‑drawn and CGI workflows—shaped the viewing experience.

Rank 5: “The Force Awakens” – Ponoc

Ponoc’s take on the opening sequence of the 2015 film feels like a nostalgic postcard rather than a fresh retelling. The studio’s signature cel‑style illustration is charming, yet the pacing drags and the story offers little beyond a visual remix. Technically, Ponoc relied on a hybrid 2D/3D pipeline that, while polished, didn’t push the boundaries set by the previous volumes. The result is a short that feels more like a homage than an innovation, earning it the lowest rank.

Rank 4: “The Rise of Skywalker” – Khara

Khara’s rendition leans heavily into a hyper‑dynamic, anime‑inspired action aesthetic. The studio’s use of real‑time motion capture and particle effects gives the battle scenes a visceral punch, but the narrative is thin, offering only a rapid montage of familiar plot beats.
