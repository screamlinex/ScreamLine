The Beatles Preview ‘Anthology 4’ With Unreleased Version of ‘I’ve Just Seen a Face’

Summary: Fab Four unearth “Take 3” of Help! classic ahead of new volume's Nov. 21 release.

Unreleased Version of I’ve Just Seen a Face

On the upcoming Anthology 4, the Beatles will debut an early take of I’ve Just Seen a Face recorded in 1964. The session features a raw vocal mix, a slightly different guitar arrangement, and a noticeably faster tempo compared to the 1965 release. The track offers listeners a glimpse into the song’s developmental stages before it appeared on the 1965 album Help!.

Take 3 of Help!

In addition to the new I’ve Just Seen a Face version, Anthology 4 includes “Take 3” of the title track from the 1965 album. This take showcases a more stripped‑down arrangement with prominent acoustic guitar and a tighter vocal delivery. The recording, captured at EMI Studios on 27 April 1965, provides insight into the Beatles’ evolving sound during the mid‑’60s.

Anthology 4 Release Details

The fourth volume of the Beatles Anthology series is scheduled for release on 21 November 2025. It will comprise 27 tracks, including demos, alternate takes, and newly discovered material from the 1963–1969 era. The collection is expected to appeal to both longtime fans and new listeners seeking a deeper understanding of the band’s creative process.

Fan and Critic Reactions

Early previews have generated excitement among Beatles enthusiasts, with many praising the clarity of the newly restored recordings. Critics highlight the historical value of the unreleased takes, noting how they illuminate the band’s experimentation during their most prolific years.
