Tokyo Fest Premiere ‘Double Happiness’ Boards Moebius for Asia-Pacific Sales

Summary: Hong Kong sales company Moebius Entertainment has secured the Asia‑Pacific distribution rights for the Taiwanese comedy‑drama *Double Happiness*, following its world premiere at the Tokyo International Film Festival. Directed by Joseph Chen‑Chieh Hsu—known for his award‑winning debut *Little Big Women*—the film stars Kuan‑Ting Liu alongside a talented ensemble that brings fresh energy to a story about family, identity, and the pursuit of happiness.

Tokyo Premiere Sets the Stage

*Double Happiness* opened the 27th Tokyo International Film Festival to enthusiastic applause, with critics praising its blend of humor and heartfelt moments. The film’s vibrant visuals and sharp dialogue captured the attention of festival programmers, setting the stage for a successful sales pitch in the competitive Asia‑Pacific market.

Moebius Entertainment’s Strategic Acquisition

Moebius Entertainment, a Hong Kong‑based sales agent with a proven track record of bringing critically acclaimed Asian cinema to global audiences, announced that it has taken on international sales for the film. “We saw the potential of *Double Happiness* to resonate across cultures,” said Moebius CEO, Li Wei. “Its universal themes paired with local specificity make it a strong contender for both theatrical releases and streaming platforms.”

Director Joseph Chen‑Chieh Hsu’s Vision

Hsu, who first made waves with the intimate drama *Little Big Women*, returns with a film that balances comedic beats with poignant social commentary. The director’s signature approach—tight character studies set against visually engaging backdrops—promises a film that is as entertaining as it is thought‑provoking. Hsu’s collaboration with a seasoned cinematographer ensures that every frame captures the film’s emotional nuance.

Stellar Cast Drives Anticipation

Kuan‑Ting Liu, celebrated for his role in *A Sun*, leads the cast with a layered performance that anchors the film’s narrative. Joining him are rising stars such as Mei‑Ling Chen, who brings a fresh comedic edge, and veteran actor Tsai Kuo‑Hsi, whose gravitas adds depth to the ensemble. The chemistry among the actors has already been highlighted in early screenings, hinting at a memorable viewing experience.
