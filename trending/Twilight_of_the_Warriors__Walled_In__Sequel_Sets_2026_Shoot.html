‘Twilight of the Warriors: Walled In’ Sequel Sets 2026 Shoot, Director Soi Cheang Reveals in Tokyo

Summary: The highly anticipated sequel to Hong Kong period action film “Twilight of the Warriors: Walled In” is slated to begin shooting in March 2026, according to director Soi Cheang, who revealed the news during a Q&A session at the Tokyo International Film Festival. Cheang also teased that a prequel will be shot immediately afterward, hinting at an ambitious, multi‑chapter saga.

Director’s Vision

Soi Cheang emphasized that the new chapter will build on the gritty realism that made the first film a cult hit, while pushing the envelope with more elaborate set pieces and a deeper exploration of the warrior culture that shaped the region. “We’re taking the world of the original and expanding it,” he told reporters. “The next film will be darker, more personal, and, most importantly, visually richer.”

Production Timeline

Filming will officially kick off on March 12, 2026, in Hong Kong’s historic districts, followed by on‑location shoots in Taiwan and mainland China. Production is slated to wrap by late August, with a post‑production schedule that includes extensive CGI work to recreate the epic battle sequences the franchise is known for. Cheang confirmed that the prequel, set to explore the origins of the warrior clans, will begin pre‑production in September of the same year, with principal photography slated to start in early 2027.

Casting and Characters

Veteran star Lau Ching‑Wai will reprise his role as the stoic warrior commander, while a fresh face—Taiwanese actor Hsieh Chieh‑hao—will join the cast as a young mercenary seeking redemption. Cheang hinted at a cameo from a legendary action star, adding an element of surprise for fans. “We’re blending the familiar with the new to keep the story fresh and engaging,” he said.

Anticipated Highlights

Audiences can expect the signature blend of hand‑to‑hand combat, large‑scale set pieces, and a soundtrack that fuses traditional Cantonese instrumentation with modern orchestration. The director also teased a “surprise twist” that will tie the sequel directly into the prequel, suggesting that the franchise is moving toward an interconnected narrative universe. With a dedicated fanbase and the momentum from the first film’s box‑office success, “Twilight of the Warriors: Walled In 2” is poised to become a landmark event in Hong Kong cinema.
