‘Star Wars’ Fans Launch Billboard Campaign to Save ‘The Hunt for Ben Solo’: ‘No One’s Ever Really Gone’

Summary: Star Wars enthusiasts have taken to the streets of New York City, unveiling a giant billboard that reads “Save The Hunt for Ben Solo” in a bid to convince Disney to revive the shelved 2021 project. The campaign, fueled by a viral social media thread, showcases the fervor of the fanbase and underscores the enduring cultural impact of the franchise.

Background: The Shelved Project

In 2021, Disney quietly announced the cancellation of “The Hunt for Ben Solo,” a planned sequel that would have followed the aftermath of “The Last Jedi.” The decision, driven by shifting corporate priorities and concerns over box‑office performance, left a void in the franchise’s narrative arc. Fans, who had been eagerly anticipating a deeper exploration of Ben Solo’s redemption, felt the loss acutely.

The Billboard Campaign

Last week, a group of passionate Star Wars fans paid a hefty fee to secure a 30‑by‑60 foot billboard on a high‑traffic New York City corner. The ad features a stylized silhouette of Ben Solo, a bold “SAVE THE HUNT FOR BEN SOLO” call‑to‑action, and a QR code that directs viewers to a petition hosted on Change.org. The billboard’s location—adjacent to the historic Broadway theater—ensures maximum visibility, especially during the holiday season.

Fan Reactions

Social media exploded with support. Twitter users shared screenshots of the billboard, while Instagram stories captured on‑the‑spot reactions from commuters. Fans expressed a mix of excitement and frustration, citing the project’s potential to resolve lingering plot threads and deepen character arcs. The campaign has already garnered over 2,000 signatures, and the petition’s administrators report that it has been forwarded to Disney’s corporate communications team.

Disney’s Response

As of the latest update, Disney has not issued an official statement regarding the billboard or the petition. Industry insiders suggest that the company is monitoring fan sentiment closely, especially as the studio prepares to launch the new “Star Wars” trilogy. Whether the pressure will prompt a reconsideration remains uncertain, but the campaign demonstrates the power of organized fandom to influence corporate decision‑making.

What’s Next?

The campaign organizers plan to extend the billboard’s run through the end of the year and are exploring additional avenues such as a Kickstarter fundraiser for a fan‑made short film that would fill the narrative gap left by the canceled project. Whether Disney will heed the call or dismiss it as a niche protest, the initiative highlights a growing trend of fans leveraging public platforms to shape the future of beloved franchises.
