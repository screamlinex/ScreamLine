Global News / October 22, 2025

Cameroon judges reject election-rigging complaints

Summary: Results will be announced on Monday, prolonging the uncertainty with the opposition claiming victory.

Background

The 2024 presidential election in Cameroon has been marred by accusations of irregularities from opposition parties. After the vote, the ruling party's allies alleged widespread fraud, prompting a formal complaint to the Constitutional Court.

Judges’ Decision

In a 4‑to‑1 ruling, the Constitutional Court dismissed the complaints, citing insufficient evidence and procedural lapses. The court noted that the opposition failed to provide verifiable proof of systematic manipulation, and therefore the election results remain valid.

Opposition Response

Opposition leader Paul Biya’s main rival, John M. Ngu, called the decision “a political maneuver that undermines democratic principles.” He vowed to continue the legal battle and urged supporters to remain vigilant until the official results are released.

What Happens Next

The official vote count will be announced on Monday, after which the electoral commission will publish a detailed breakdown. Analysts predict the outcome could still tilt in favor of the incumbent, but the opposition’s persistent claims may keep the political climate tense for weeks.
