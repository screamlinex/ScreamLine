Global News / October 23, 2025

Ten dead in blast at Russian munitions plant in Urals

Summary: A massive explosion at the Plastmass military munitions plant in Kopeisk, Ural region, has killed 10 workers and injured dozens more. Witnesses reported a fireball and a series of detonations that shattered windows across the town. The incident has prompted an emergency response from local authorities and raised concerns about safety protocols at Russia’s strategic weapons facilities.

Explosion Details

The blast occurred around 08:45 local time, when a storage bunker reportedly ignited after a malfunction in the handling equipment. Firefighters described a towering plume of smoke and a shockwave that caused structural damage to surrounding buildings. Preliminary investigations suggest that the explosion was caused by a rapid detonation of a stockpile of high‑explosive ordnance.

Casualties and Damage

According to the Ural Regional Health Department, 10 employees have been confirmed dead, with 27 others sustaining injuries ranging from minor burns to severe trauma. The plant’s main production line was heavily damaged, and the surrounding residential area suffered broken windows and collapsed roofs.

Official Response

President Vladimir Putin has ordered a full investigation into the incident and has directed the Ministry of Defense to review safety protocols at all munitions facilities. The local governor has declared a state of emergency and requested federal assistance for rescue and medical operations.

Historical Context

Plastmass has been a key supplier of artillery shells and other munitions since the Soviet era, with a history of several smaller accidents. This latest blast marks the most severe incident in the plant’s recent operational history.

Reactions

Industry experts warn that the incident underscores the risks of aging infrastructure and insufficient safety oversight. Internationally, the United Nations has expressed concern and called for transparent reporting on the incident’s cause.
