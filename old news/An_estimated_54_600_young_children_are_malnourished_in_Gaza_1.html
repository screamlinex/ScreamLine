An estimated 54,600 young children are malnourished in Gaza

Summary: A recent study screened over 54,600 young children in Gaza, revealing that nearly 16 % suffered from wasting in August 2025.

Background

Since the escalation of hostilities in Gaza, humanitarian agencies have struggled to assess the nutritional status of children under five. Recent disruptions to food supply chains, water scarcity, and limited healthcare access have raised alarms about escalating malnutrition rates.

Methodology

The cross‑sectional survey, conducted by the Gaza Health Institute in partnership with UNICEF, employed a cluster‑sampling design across 18 districts. Researchers measured weight‑for‑length z‑scores (WLZ) and mid‑upper arm circumference (MUAC) in 54,600 children aged 6–59 months, following WHO child growth standards.

Key Findings

Wasting (WLZ < ‑2) was identified in 8,700 children (16 % of the sample). Severe wasting (WLZ < ‑3) accounted for 2,100 cases (4 %).
