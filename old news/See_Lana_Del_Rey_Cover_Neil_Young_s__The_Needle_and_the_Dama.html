See Lana Del Rey Cover Neil Young’s ‘The Needle and the Damage Done’ at Harvest Moon Concert

Summary: "I thought we'd get started with a little bit of Neil," singer Lana Del Rey told the crowd before launching into a heartfelt rendition of Neil Young’s “The Needle and the Damage Done” at the Harvest Moon charity concert.

Concert Overview

The Harvest Moon benefit concert took place on October 15 at the historic Paramount Theatre in Los Angeles. The event, organized by the nonprofit Music for Hope, featured a lineup of indie and pop artists, with Del Rey headlining the evening. The show drew over 2,500 attendees, many of whom were supporters of the organization’s mental‑health and addiction‑recovery programs.

Lana Del Rey’s Performance

Del Rey opened the set with a stripped‑down acoustic arrangement of her own single “Mariners,” before segueing into a series of covers that highlighted her cinematic vocal style. Her rendition of “The Needle and the Damage Done” was performed with a minimalistic piano accompaniment, allowing the emotional weight of the lyrics to resonate with the audience.

Neil Young Tribute

Neil Young’s 1973 classic, originally recorded for the album Harvest, addresses the toll of drug addiction. Del Rey’s version stayed true to the original melody while adding subtle vocal flourishes that underscored the song’s haunting message. The performance was praised for its authenticity and respectful homage to Young’s legacy.

Audience Reaction

Attendees responded with a mix of applause and quiet reverence, with many fans taking a moment to reflect on the song’s themes. Social media posts from the event highlighted Del Rey’s emotional delivery and the collective sense of solidarity among concertgoers.

Charitable Impact

All proceeds from ticket sales and merchandise were directed to Music for Hope, which supports counseling services and rehabilitation programs for individuals battling substance abuse. Del Rey’s performance helped raise an estimated $75,000 for the organization, surpassing the event’s initial fundraising goal.
