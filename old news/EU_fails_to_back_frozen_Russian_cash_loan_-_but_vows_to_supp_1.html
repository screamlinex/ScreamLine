Global News / October 24, 2025

EU fails to back frozen Russian cash loan - but vows to support Ukraine

Summary: EU leaders postponed the decision on the frozen Russian cash loan until December after Belgium raised concerns over the legal framework and potential diplomatic fallout, while reaffirming their commitment to supporting Ukraine with financial and political backing.

EU Decision Delayed

The European Council, convened in Brussels, voted to defer the final approval of the 500 million‑euro loan secured by frozen Russian assets. The move came after a last‑minute objection from the Belgian delegation, which argued that the legal basis for the loan could be challenged in international courts and might jeopardise the EU’s standing in global finance.

Belgium’s Concerns

Belgium’s Foreign Minister highlighted that the loan’s structure could contravene the EU’s own sanctions regime and that any misstep could undermine the credibility of the bloc’s financial mechanisms. He called for a comprehensive legal review before the EU proceeds with the transaction.

Impact on Ukraine

Despite the delay, EU officials reiterated that the loan would still bolster Ukraine’s war‑time budget. The funds, once released, will be earmarked for military aid, reconstruction projects, and economic stabilization programmes aimed at mitigating the humanitarian toll of the conflict.

Future Outlook

EU leaders are scheduled to reconvene in December to revisit the loan proposal, with a focus on tightening the legal safeguards and ensuring compliance with international norms. In the interim, the bloc continues to expand its sanctions arsenal and provide direct financial assistance to Ukraine, signalling a steadfast, if cautious, approach to the crisis.
