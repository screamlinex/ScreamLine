NFL Commissioner Backs Bad Bunny’s Super Bowl Show as Conservatives Keep Melting Down

Summary: NFL Commissioner Roger Goodell said the league expects the Super Bowl halftime show featuring Bad Bunny to be an exciting and united moment. He also ruled out replacing the Puerto Rican performer amid rising conservative backlash.

Commissioner’s Statement

During a press briefing on Monday, Goodell emphasized that the NFL’s goal is to deliver a “memorable and inclusive” event. He noted that the league has no plans to replace Bad Bunny, citing contractual obligations and fan interest.

Bad Bunny’s Performance

The superstar will headline the 2025 Super Bowl halftime show, joining a lineup that includes several top pop and hip‑hop acts. Bad Bunny’s set is slated to feature a mix of his chart‑topping hits and a collaboration with a leading American artist.

Public Reaction

Viewership projections for the halftime show have risen, with analysts predicting a 12% increase in audience share compared to last year. Social media buzz shows a split, with many fans praising the cultural representation.

Conservative Criticism

Conservative commentators have called the selection “unpatriotic” and urged the NFL to remove the performer. Goodell responded that the league’s focus remains on the game and that the halftime show is a celebration of music, not politics.

Impact on NFL

Officials expect the show to boost ticket sales and merchandise for the 2026 season. The NFL’s marketing team has already begun planning cross‑promotional campaigns featuring Bad Bunny’s brand.
