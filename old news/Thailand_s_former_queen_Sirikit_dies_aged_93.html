Global News / October 25, 2025

Thailand's former queen Sirikit dies aged 93

Summary: Thailand’s former queen, Sirikit, passed away peacefully on Friday night at the age of 93, according to an official statement from the Thai Royal Household.

Background

Born in 1932, Princess Sirikit became queen consort upon her marriage to King Bhumibol Adulyadej in 1950. Over her 70-year reign, she was celebrated for her philanthropic work, particularly in education, health, and rural development, earning her the affectionate title “Mother of the Nation.”

Royal Household Statement

In a brief but heartfelt announcement, the Royal Household confirmed that Queen Sirikit died peacefully in her sleep at the Grand Palace. The statement noted her “deep devotion to the people of Thailand” and expressed gratitude for her “endless service and unwavering support of the monarchy.”

Public Reaction

The news triggered an outpouring of condolences across the country. Thousands of citizens gathered in Bangkok’s Chao Phraya River to offer flowers and prayers. Social media platforms were flooded with tributes from fans, politicians, and international dignitaries, many recalling her iconic fashion sense and humanitarian legacy.

Legacy and Memorials

Queen Sirikit’s legacy includes the Sirikit National Convention Center and the Sirikit Foundation, both of which continue to support community projects nationwide. Plans for a state funeral are underway, with a memorial service expected to take place at the Royal Thai Army Stadium, honoring her lifelong commitment to the welfare of Thai citizens.
