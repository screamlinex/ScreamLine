A rice weevil frozen in flight won the 2025 Nikon Small World photo contest

Summary: From fluorescent ferns to sprawling neurons, this year’s winning photos reveal the structures and artistry of life seen through a microscope.

2025 Nikon Small World Contest: A New Frontier in Microphotography

The Nikon Small World competition, known for spotlighting the hidden beauty of the microscopic world, welcomed over 4,000 submissions from 80 countries in 2025. Judges evaluated images on technical excellence, scientific insight, and artistic impact. The contest’s theme, “Hidden Life,” encouraged participants to capture the unseen complexity that defines our planet’s ecosystems.

Winner Spotlight: A Rice Weevil in Mid‑Flight

Photographer Dr. Elena Kovalev’s image of a rice weevil (Sitophilus oryzae) captured in the act of flight—frozen mid‑air—earned the top prize. Using a Nikon Z7 II coupled with a 100× objective and a high‑speed CMOS sensor, she recorded the insect’s wingbeats at 1,000 frames per second, revealing micro‑structures that were previously invisible to the naked eye.
