Global News / October 27, 2025

India and China resume direct flights as ties improve

Summary: The flight landed in Guangzhou on Monday morning, a milestone in the thawing of bilateral ties.

Historic Reconnection

Indian Airlines' inaugural direct service to Guangzhou took off from New Delhi at 06:15 local time, arriving in the Pearl River Delta at 09:45. The 5,400‑km journey, which once required a stopover in Bangkok, marks the first nonstop flight between the two nations since 2013.

Diplomatic Backdrop

The resumption follows a series of high‑level meetings, including the recent summit between Prime Minister Narendra Modi and President Xi Jinping. Both leaders cited the new air link as a tangible sign of improved relations and a step toward broader economic cooperation.

Economic Implications

Direct flights are expected to boost tourism, with projections of 300,000 Indian visitors to China in the next fiscal year. Trade analysts also point to a 15% increase in bilateral trade volume, driven by easier logistics for businesses on both sides.

Passenger Experience

Passengers reported a smooth journey, with complimentary Chinese cuisine and bilingual flight attendants. The airline has announced a loyalty program partnership with a major Chinese travel agency to offer exclusive discounts to frequent flyers.

Looking Ahead

Both governments have signaled plans to add a second daily flight by the end of the year, and to explore routes to other major Indian cities such as Mumbai and Kolkata. Industry insiders predict that this will set a new precedent for regional connectivity in the Indo‑Pacific.
