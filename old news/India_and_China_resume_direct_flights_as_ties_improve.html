Global News / October 27, 2025

India and China resume direct flights as ties improve

Summary: The flight landed in Guangzhou on Monday morning, marking a milestone in the thawing of bilateral ties between India and China.

Historical Context

For decades, political tensions and border disputes had kept India and China from operating direct commercial flights. The last time a commercial aircraft flew non-stop between the two capitals was in the early 1990s. The resumption of direct services signals a significant shift in diplomatic relations, echoing the broader trend of easing hostilities that began with the 2023 border ceasefire agreements.

Economic Impact

Direct flights will cut travel time by more than six hours, opening up new opportunities for business travel, tourism, and cargo exchange. Analysts estimate that the new route could generate up to $500 million in bilateral trade value over the next five years, with airlines expecting a 12% increase in passenger revenue on the Shanghai–New Delhi corridor alone.

Political Significance

Beyond economics, the flight is a symbolic gesture of confidence. Prime Minister Narendra Modi and President Xi Jinping both highlighted the route during a joint press conference, framing it as a “new chapter” in Indo‑Chinese relations. The move also aligns with the recent signing of a joint airspace management agreement, which will streamline flight operations and enhance safety.

Future Outlook

Industry experts predict that the success of the Guangzhou–New Delhi service could pave the way for additional direct links to other major Indian cities such as Mumbai, Kolkata, and Hyderabad. Meanwhile, the Indian government is reportedly negotiating with Chinese carriers to expand cargo capacity, potentially boosting supply chains for high‑tech and pharmaceutical sectors.
