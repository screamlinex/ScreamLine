See Comet Lemmon cross paths with a cosmic serpent tonight

Summary: Comet C/2025 A6 (Lemmon) will pass through the neck of the great celestial serpent represented by the constellation Serpens Caput on the nights of Oct. 26‑27.

Celestial Encounter

The faint comet will cross the bright, serpent‑shaped constellation Serpens Caput, creating a fleeting alignment that astronomers call a “cross‑path.” The comet’s tail will trail across the sky, offering a spectacular contrast against the constellation’s dark background. Observers can track the comet’s progress with binoculars or a small telescope; its apparent magnitude will brighten to about 6.5 near the apex of the crossing.

Where to Look

Serpens Caput is best viewed from the Northern Hemisphere in late autumn. The comet will be located near the star Zubenelgenubi (α Serpens) during the first half of the night, moving westward toward the constellation Ophiuchus. A clear, dark sky at a site with minimal light pollution will provide the best viewing experience. Mobile apps such as SkySafari or Stellarium can help you pinpoint the exact position in real time.

Scientific Significance

Comet C/2025 A6 was discovered in early 2025 by the Lemmon Observatory survey. Its orbit takes it from the distant Oort Cloud into the inner solar system, offering a unique opportunity to study pristine cometary material. During the Serpens crossing, spectroscopic instruments can measure the comet’s composition as it approaches the Sun, revealing insights into early solar system chemistry.

Safety and Tips

While the comet itself poses no danger, be mindful of the bright stars in Serpens that can confuse casual stargazers. Use a star chart or a planetarium app to confirm the comet’s position. Bring a red‑light flashlight to preserve night vision, and consider a light, breathable jacket, as temperatures can drop in the evening.
