Global News / October 26, 2025

Rebel group claims capture of key city in Sudan

Summary: The fall of el‑Fasher would cement the RSF's control of the west and reinforce a de facto split in the nation.

Background

El‑Fasher, the capital of North Kordofan, sits on a strategic crossroads that links the western provinces to the oil‑rich areas of the east. For months, the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces (RSF) have been advancing westward, targeting key towns to isolate the government’s forces in Khartoum and its eastern flank.

The city’s capture would not only secure the RSF’s logistical hub but also give them a foothold to launch further operations into the heartland of Sudan.

RSF's Tactics

Sources close to the RSF describe the assault as a coordinated push involving armored vehicles, artillery, and a swarm of drones. Intelligence reports indicate that the forces bypassed the main defensive line by exploiting a weak corridor along the Nahr Marai River.

Once inside the city, RSF units reportedly seized the central police station and the main market, using them as staging points to consolidate control.

Local Impact

Residents of el‑Fasher report a sudden shift in power dynamics, with RSF troops establishing checkpoints and a curfew. The city’s infrastructure—particularly the main power grid—has reportedly been cut off to prevent any coordinated resistance.

Humanitarian agencies warn of a looming crisis as displaced people flood into the city, straining an already overburdened medical system.

International Reaction

The United Nations and the African Union have called for an immediate ceasefire and urged all parties to respect civilian protection. Meanwhile, the United States and European Union have issued statements urging restraint, though they have not yet imposed new sanctions.

Regional actors, including Ethiopia and Egypt, are monitoring the situation closely, as a shift in control could alter the balance of power in the Horn of Africa.
