Global News / October 26, 2025

Rebel group claims capture of key city in Sudan

Summary: The fall of el‑Fasher would cement the RSF's control of the west and reinforces a de facto split in the nation.

RSF Advances into El‑Fasher

The Rapid Support Forces (RSF) announced on Tuesday that they had seized the strategic town of el‑Fasher in western Sudan, a move that could shift the balance of power in the ongoing civil war.

Strategic Significance of El‑Fasher

El‑Fasher sits on key transport routes between Khartoum and the western provinces. Control of the town would grant the RSF a logistical advantage and a foothold for further incursions into the south.

Government Response and Counter‑Claims

President Abdel Fattah al‑Bashir’s government dismissed the RSF’s claim, calling the announcement “propaganda” and urging citizens to stay calm.

International Community Watches Closely

The United Nations and the African Union have urged restraint, warning that the escalation could destabilise the entire region. Western diplomats have called for a rapid ceasefire.

Implications for Sudan’s Future

Analysts say that if the RSF consolidates its hold on the west, a de facto partition of Sudan could become reality, complicating efforts to negotiate a comprehensive peace settlement.
