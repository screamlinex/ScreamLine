Kingsley Ben-Adir and Archie Madekwe to Play Brothers in Bijan Sheibani’s ‘The Arrival’ for ‘Conclave’ Producers, Michael Fassbender’s DMC Film

Summary: Rising British stars Kingsley Ben‑Adir and Archie Madekwe are set to play brothers in Bijan Sheibani’s feature directorial debut, The Arrival. The film, produced by Michael Fassbender’s DMC Film and Conclave, also features Joanna Scanlan and Alex Descas in key supporting roles.

Cast & Characters

Ben‑Adir, known for his turn in Barbie and Bob Marley: One Love, will portray the older brother, a seasoned activist grappling with family expectations. Madekwe, who earned acclaim in Saltburn and Lurker, takes on the younger brother, a tech‑savvy idealist whose ambitions collide with tradition. Supporting performances include Joanna Scanlan as the matriarch, whose layered portrayal adds depth to the family dynamic, and Alex Descas as a charismatic community organizer.

Production & Direction

Bijan Sheibani, an award‑winning theatre director, steps into the director’s chair for the first time on screen. His theatrical background informs the film’s intimate, character‑driven storytelling. Production is a joint venture between Conclave and DMC Film, with Fassbender’s production company providing both creative and financial backing. Filming took place across London and Manchester, capturing the city’s gritty realism and vibrant subcultures.

Plot Overview

Set against the backdrop of contemporary Britain, The Arrival follows two brothers as they navigate the complexities of identity, heritage, and belonging. The story explores themes of migration, community activism, and generational conflict, with the siblings’ relationship at the heart of the narrative. The film blends sharp dialogue with visually striking scenes that highlight the city’s evolving cultural landscape.

Release & Distribution

The film is slated for a limited theatrical release in the UK in late 2025, followed by a wider rollout in North America and Europe. DMC Film will handle global distribution, while Conclave will focus on the UK market. A companion digital release is planned for streaming platforms in early 2026, ensuring accessibility to a broad audience.
