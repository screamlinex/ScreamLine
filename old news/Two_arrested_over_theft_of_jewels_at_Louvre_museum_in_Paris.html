Global News / October 26, 2025

Two arrested over theft of jewels at Louvre museum in Paris

Summary: Four thieves broke into the world's most-visited museum in broad daylight, stealing eight items worth €88m (£76m; $102m).

The Heist

On a sunny Thursday, a group of four armed men breached the Louvre's security perimeter, slipping past the museum's high‑tech alarms and cameras. Within minutes, they stole eight priceless jewels, including a diamond‑encrusted necklace, a ruby brooch, a gold crown, a sapphire tiara, a pearl bracelet, a ruby ring, a diamond brooch and a jade pendant, totaling an estimated €88 million.

Arrests Made

French police arrested two suspects, aged 32 and 38, in a nearby apartment on the 12th arrondissement. Both men were found in possession of stolen jewelry and a set of stolen passports. The police say the other two thieves are still at large and are being traced through CCTV footage and forensic evidence.

Museum Response

The Louvre has temporarily closed the galleries where the jewels were displayed. Director Alain Bernard said the museum is cooperating fully with investigators and will review its security protocols. “We are deeply shocked by this breach,” he told reporters. “We are committed to restoring the trust of our visitors and safeguarding our collections.”
