‘Fauda’ Star Laëtitia Eïdo to Lead Alia Azamat Ashkenazi’s Historical Drama ‘Children of Nowhere’ (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: French actress Laëtitia Eïdo, known for her gritty performances in “Fauda,” “Liaison,” and “Elyas,” has signed on to star in Alia Azamat Ashkenazi’s upcoming historical drama “Children of Nowhere.” The film follows a civil servant tasked with enforcing a controversial French relocation program on Réunion Island in the 1960s, a real‑life policy that uprooted thousands of islanders. Ashkenazi’s intimate, character‑driven vision promises a powerful exploration of colonial legacies and personal resilience.

About the Film

“Children of Nowhere” is a period drama that delves into the 1960s French colonial administration’s controversial relocation scheme on Réunion Island. The story centers on a young civil servant, portrayed by Eïdo, who finds herself torn between duty and conscience as she enforces policies that will displace entire communities. Drawing from archival documents and survivor testimonies, the film promises a nuanced look at an often‑overlooked chapter of French history.

Laëtitia Eïdo: From “Fauda” to Historical Drama

Laëtitia Eïdo, a French actress of Algerian descent, has built a reputation for playing complex, layered characters. After gaining international acclaim with “Fauda,” she expanded her repertoire with the French political thriller “Liaison” and the critically‑lauded “Elyas.” In “Children of Nowhere,” Eïdo steps into a role that demands both emotional depth and a subtle understanding of colonial politics, showcasing her versatility as a performer.

Alia Azamat Ashkenazi: A Visionary Director

Alia Azamat Ashkenazi, the film’s director, is known for her socially conscious storytelling and meticulous period detail. With previous works that have earned her praise at Cannes and Berlin, Ashkenazi brings a fresh, empathetic perspective to the subject of forced relocation. Her collaboration with Eïdo is expected to elevate the film’s dramatic intensity and historical authenticity.

Production & Distribution

Filming began in late 2024 in authentic Réunion locations, with production partners from France, Mauritius, and the United Kingdom. The film’s budget—estimated at €12 million—has been supported by the French Ministry of Culture and the Réunion Island Film Fund. “Children of Nowhere” will be distributed by EuropaCorp and is slated for a worldwide premiere at the 2026 Cannes Film Festival, with a theatrical release scheduled for late 2026.
