Global News / October 24, 2025

Trump says trade talks with Canada 'terminated' over anti-tariffs advert

Summary: President Donald Trump announced that trade negotiations with Canada have been terminated following a controversy over an anti‑tariff advertisement featuring former President Ronald Reagan.

The Advert That Sparked the Rift

Last month, a Canadian campaign aired an advert that quoted Ronald Reagan criticizing tariffs as a threat to economic prosperity. The clip, intended to highlight the perils of protectionism, was broadcast across multiple platforms during the U.S. presidential campaign season. The ad was seen by many as an attempt to influence U.S. policy and was met with immediate backlash from the Trump administration.

President Trump's Decision

In a brief statement released via the White House press office, President Trump declared that the U.S. had “terminated” all ongoing trade talks with Canada. He cited the ad’s “disrespectful use of the former president’s voice” and its “unwarranted attack on U.S. tariffs” as the primary reasons for the abrupt halt. Trump emphasized that the U.S. would not entertain any negotiations that involved political messaging aimed at domestic audiences.

Canadian Response

Canadian officials expressed surprise and disappointment at the decision, calling it “unprecedented” and “counterproductive.” Prime Minister Justin Trudeau urged the U.S. to reconsider, stressing that the trade relationship is vital for both economies. The Canadian Trade Minister released a statement promising to engage in dialogue once the political climate had cooled.

Implications for Trade

With the termination of talks, both sides risk a slowdown in negotiations over the U.S.–Canada–Mexico Agreement (USMCA) and potential tariff escalations. Analysts warn that prolonged stalemates could hurt U.S. exporters in Canada and Canadian farmers seeking access to the American market. The move also signals a shift toward more politicized trade policy, potentially affecting future agreements with other partners.

Looking Ahead

Observers will watch for any diplomatic outreach that might reopen channels of communication. The Trump administration’s next steps—whether to resume talks under new terms or to pursue unilateral tariffs—remain unclear. Meanwhile, both governments are likely to focus on maintaining essential trade flows while navigating the fallout from this sudden rupture.
