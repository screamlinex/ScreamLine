Marc Almond Reveals Soft Cell Finished New Album Days Before Dave Ball’s Death

Summary: Marc Almond confirmed that Soft Cell completed a new album shortly before the death of co-founder Dave Ball, expressing relief that Ball had listened to the finished record and praised its quality.

Background

Soft Cell, the 1980s synth-pop duo known for hits like “Tainted Love,” had been working on a new studio album since late 2022. The project, slated for a 2024 release, marked the first full-length collaboration between Almond and Ball since their 1984 debut.

Album Completion

Almond disclosed that the recording sessions wrapped up on March 29, 2023, just 12 days before Ball’s sudden passing on April 10, 2023. He noted that the final mix was completed and ready for distribution when Ball died.

Almond’s Reflections

“I can take solace that he heard this finished record and felt it was a great piece of work,” Almond wrote in a statement to music journalists. He added that Ball’s approval meant the duo’s final collaboration would be remembered with pride.

Reactions from the Music Community

Industry figures, including former producer Ian Stanley, praised the album’s production and lyrical depth. Fans on social media shared tributes, calling the record “a fitting farewell” to Ball’s legacy.

Legacy and Release Plans

Soft Cell’s official website announced that the album will be released digitally on September 15, 2024, with a limited vinyl edition to follow. A tribute concert is scheduled for early 2025 to honor Ball’s contributions to electronic music.
