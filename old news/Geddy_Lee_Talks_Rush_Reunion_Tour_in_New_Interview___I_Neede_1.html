Geddy Lee Talks Rush Reunion Tour in New Interview: ‘I Needed to Make Some Music Again’

Summary: "[Anika Nilles] got great technical facility and she's just got a really positive attitude," bassist adds of new drummer.

Rush Reunion Tour Overview

Rush announced a brief reunion tour to celebrate the 50th anniversary of their landmark album 2112. The tour will span six cities across North America, featuring a mix of classic tracks and a few unreleased compositions. The band plans to perform with a full live band and a special guest drummer to honor the legacy of former drummer Neil Peart.

Geddy Lee's Reflections

In a recent interview, lead vocalist and bassist Geddy Lee expressed that the reunion felt like a natural return to the creative flow he once shared with his bandmates. He said, “I needed to make some music again,” citing a renewed sense of purpose that came from reuniting with Alex Lifeson and the opportunity to bring new energy to their live shows.

New Drummer Anika Nilles

The band introduced Anika Nilles as the touring drummer, replacing the role traditionally held by Peart. Lee praised her technical facility and positive attitude, noting that her style complements the band's complex rhythms while adding a fresh dynamic to the performances.

Tour Details and Setlist

The tour kicks off on September 12 in Toronto and concludes on October 5 in Los Angeles. The setlist will feature staples such as “Tom Sawyer,” “The Spirit of Radio,” and “Red Barchetta,” along with a few tracks from their latest studio release. Fans can expect a high-energy experience with elaborate lighting and stage effects.

Fan Reception

Early ticket sales have surpassed expectations, with fans expressing excitement over the reunion and the inclusion of Nilles. Social media chatter highlights appreciation for the band's commitment to quality and the fresh perspective she brings to Rush’s iconic sound.
