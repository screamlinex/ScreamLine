Korean Action-Comedy ‘Boss’ Sells to Multiple Territories After Box Office Success

Summary: Korean sales powerhouse Finecut has secured multiple international distribution deals for the hit action‑comedy “Boss.” With over 2.3 million admissions domestically and ranking as the fifth highest‑grossing Korean film of 2025, the film’s global reach now spans Asia, the Middle East, and Latin America, thanks to partners such as Abnormal Studios (Cambodia), Eastbound Films (Thailand), and Global Box (UAE).

Box Office Success

“Boss” opened on 1,200 screens across South Korea, pulling in a record‑breaking 1.8 million tickets in its first two weeks. The film’s blend of high‑octane action and sharp humor resonated with audiences, propelling it to 2.3 million admissions by the end of the year. In the domestic market alone, it generated a gross of approximately $28 million, cementing its place among the top Korean releases of 2025.

International Sales Deals

Finecut’s recent negotiations have seen “Boss” snapped up by a slate of distributors across multiple territories. In Southeast Asia, Abnormal Studios will handle release in Cambodia, while Eastbound Films will distribute in Thailand and Vietnam. The Middle East market is covered by Global Box, which will release the film in the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar. In Latin America, CineVista will manage distribution in Brazil and Mexico, with plans for a wider rollout across the region.

Key Distributors and Territories

Abnormal Studios, known for its strong presence in the Cambodian market, will launch “Boss” in early November, aiming to capture the region’s growing appetite for Korean cinema. Eastbound Films will release the film in Thailand’s 3D theaters, while Global Box will coordinate a simultaneous multi‑screen launch in the UAE, capitalizing on the country’s increasing demand for foreign blockbusters. CineVista’s partnership will bring “Boss” to Latin America’s largest markets, with a focus on digital streaming platforms following the theatrical run.

Future Prospects

With the film’s domestic success and a robust distribution network in place, “Boss” is poised for a strong global performance. Finecut plans to leverage the film’s cross‑genre appeal to secure streaming rights on major platforms such as Netflix and Disney+ in the coming months. Additionally, the success of this project is expected to open doors for future Korean action‑comedy collaborations, further expanding the genre’s international footprint.
