See Jackson Browne, Dwight Yoakam Cover Warren Zevon at Tribute Concert

Summary: At a Los Angeles tribute concert, Jackson Browne, Dwight Yoakam, and other prominent musicians honored Warren Zevon, a future Rock Hall inductee, with powerful covers of his hits.

Tribute Concert Highlights

The evening took place at the historic Hollywood Bowl, drawing an enthusiastic crowd of Zevon fans and music lovers. Organizers highlighted the event as a celebration of Zevon’s songwriting legacy and his influence on contemporary artists.

Featured Performers

Jackson Browne opened with a soulful rendition of “Warren’s Song.” Dwight Yoakam followed with a high‑energy cover of “The Hunted.” Other notable appearances included Fountains of Wayne, Marshall Crenshaw, Shooter Jennings, and the band The Black Crowes, each delivering standout performances of Zevon classics.

Song Covers

The setlist featured a mix of Zevon’s most celebrated tracks, including “Werewolves of London,” “Roland the Headless Thompson Gunner,” and “Reuben and the Dark.” Each artist brought their unique style while staying true to Zevon’s original compositions.

Acknowledging Zevon’s Legacy

Throughout the concert, speakers reflected on Zevon’s contributions to rock and folk music, noting his sharp lyricism and his role in shaping the genre. The event served as a prelude to his forthcoming Rock Hall induction, underscoring his enduring impact.
