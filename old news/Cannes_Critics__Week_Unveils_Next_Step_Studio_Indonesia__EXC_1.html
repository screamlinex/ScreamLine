Cannes Critics’ Week Unveils Next Step Studio Indonesia (EXCLUSIVE)

Summary: Cannes Critics’ Week has unveiled Next Step Studio Indonesia, marking a significant expansion of its talent development programs for 2026. The venture, created in partnership with production companies KawanKawan Media and DW, revives the spirit of La Factory – a filmmaker incubator that ran at Directors’ Fortnight beginning in 2013. Under the new banner, four emerging Indonesian filmmakers will receive mentorship, funding and festival exposure, positioning the country as a rising hub for cinematic innovation.

The Launch at Cannes

During the opening ceremony of Critics’ Week, director of the festival, Jean‑Pierre Boulanger, announced the launch of Next Step Studio Indonesia. “We are thrilled to bring a new platform that nurtures the next generation of Indonesian storytellers,” Boulanger said. The announcement was met with applause from a packed audience, underscoring the festival’s commitment to global diversity.

Strategic Partnerships

Next Step Studio Indonesia is a joint venture between KawanKawan Media, a leading Indonesian production house, and DW, the German broadcaster with a strong film production arm. The collaboration blends local expertise with international resources, ensuring that selected filmmakers have access to cutting‑edge technology, professional coaching, and distribution networks.

Program Structure and Selection

Four filmmakers will be chosen through a competitive application process that evaluates originality, cultural relevance, and technical skill. The program, running from March to September 2026, includes workshops on screenwriting, cinematography, and post‑production, as well as one‑on‑one mentorship with industry veterans. Participants will also receive a €10,000 production grant to support their projects.

Impact on Indonesian Cinema

Industry analysts predict that the studio will accelerate the international visibility of Indonesian cinema. “With Cannes as a launchpad, these filmmakers can reach audiences worldwide and attract co‑production deals,” noted film critic Anisa Rahman. The initiative also aims to foster cross‑cultural collaborations, encouraging Indonesian stories to resonate with global viewers.

Future Outlook

Looking ahead, Critics’ Week plans to evaluate the program’s success by tracking festival entries, distribution deals, and awards garnered by the studio’s alumni. “This is just the beginning,” Boulanger said. “Next Step Studio Indonesia will set a precedent for how film festivals can actively shape the next wave of cinematic talent.”
