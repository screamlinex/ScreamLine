See Jackson Browne, Dwight Yoakam Cover Warren Zevon at Tribute Concert

Summary: A Los Angeles tribute concert honoring late singer‑songwriter Warren Zevon featured performances by Jackson Browne, Dwight Yoakam, Fountains of Wayne, Marshall Crenshaw, Shooter Jennings, and others. The event celebrated Zevon’s legacy ahead of his Rock Hall induction.

Tribute Concert Overview

The concert took place on Saturday, November 9 at the iconic Hollywood Bowl. It was organized by Zevon’s close friends and former collaborators, aiming to showcase the breadth of his influence across rock, country, and pop genres.

Notable Performers

Jackson Browne opened the show with a heartfelt rendition of “Warren’s Song,” while Dwight Yoakam delivered a gritty version of “The Hometown Anthem.” Fountains of Wayne paid homage with a high‑energy cover of “I Don’t Want to Be a Soldier.” Marshall Crenshaw and Shooter Jennings joined forces for a duet on “The Long Road.” The lineup also included contributions from The Dead Weather, The Avett Brothers, and a surprise appearance by Garth Brooks.

Setlist Highlights

Key tracks performed included “The Letter,” “Rehab,” “The Good Side,” and the crowd‑favorite “The End of the World.” Several artists incorporated personal anecdotes about Zevon, adding an intimate layer to the set. The final encore featured a communal sing‑along to “Warren’s Song,” drawing a standing ovation from the audience.

Venue and Timing

Held at the Hollywood Bowl, the venue’s 17,500‑seat capacity filled to the rafters. The concert ran from 7:00 p.m. to 10:30 p.m., with a brief intermission. Ticket sales were limited to 5,000 seats, and all proceeds were donated to the Warren Zevon Fund, supporting music education and mental health initiatives.
