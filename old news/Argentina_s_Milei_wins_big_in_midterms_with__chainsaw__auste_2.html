Argentina's Milei wins big in midterms with 'chainsaw' austerity

Summary: Milei's radical spending cuts have won favour with Donald Trump, but also attracted widespread criticism.

Election Results

Argentina’s midterm elections saw libertarian economist and former TV personality Javier Milei secure a landslide victory in the Senate, capturing 57% of the vote in a country long dominated by traditional parties. Analysts say Milei’s “chainsaw austerity” platform resonated with voters tired of inflation and fiscal mismanagement.

Chainsaw Austerity Explained

Dubbed “chainsaw austerity” by critics, Milei’s plan calls for slashing public spending by 30% over five years, eliminating subsidies, and privatizing key state enterprises. The goal: reduce the fiscal deficit from 10% of GDP to under 2% while boosting investor confidence.

While proponents argue the cuts will stabilize the economy, opponents warn that the policy could deepen inequality and erode social safety nets.

International Reactions

U.S. President Donald Trump publicly praised Milei’s boldness, tweeting, “Argentina’s new leader is a true free‑market champion—great to see!” The tweet sparked a flurry of commentary among global business circles, with many investors eyeing Argentina’s revamped regulatory environment.

Conversely, the International Monetary Fund cautioned that the austerity measures might trigger a recession if not paired with growth‑stimulating reforms.

Domestic Response

While Milei enjoys a growing base among the middle class and entrepreneurs, labor unions and left‑wing parties have staged protests, accusing the president of “economic violence.” A recent strike by public sector workers threatens to derail the rollout of the austerity program.

Opinion polls indicate that while 68% of voters approve of Milei’s economic agenda, only 32% believe it will improve living standards for the average Argentine.
