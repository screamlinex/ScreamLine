Global News / October 23, 2025

Trump says 'existing structure' of White House East Wing to be torn down

Summary: President Trump announced that the existing structure of the White House East Wing will be demolished to make way for a new $250 million ballroom, a move that contradicts his earlier statement that the addition would not interfere with the current building.

Trump's New White House Vision

In a recent press briefing, President Trump outlined a bold plan to transform the White House’s East Wing into a grand ballroom capable of hosting state functions and lavish events. He described the project as a “modern, luxurious addition” that would “enhance the dignity and prestige of the executive mansion.”

The East Wing Demolition Plans

The president’s team has identified the existing East Wing, which houses the President’s offices, as the site for demolition. The proposal includes a full reconstruction of the wing, with the new ballroom set to replace the current space entirely. Trump emphasized that the project would be completed within a tight 18‑month schedule and would be funded through a private‑sector partnership.

Reactions from Preservationists

Historic preservation advocates have voiced strong opposition, citing the East Wing’s architectural significance and the potential loss of irreplaceable artifacts. “This is a reckless decision that threatens the integrity of the White House,” said the National Trust for Historic Preservation. The group is urging the administration to reconsider the demolition and explore alternative renovation options.

Implications for the White House

If the demolition proceeds, the White House could see a major shift in its interior layout and public accessibility. The new ballroom would likely become a centerpiece for diplomatic ceremonies, but critics argue that the cost—estimated at $250 million—could outweigh the benefits, especially given the ongoing debates about federal spending priorities.
