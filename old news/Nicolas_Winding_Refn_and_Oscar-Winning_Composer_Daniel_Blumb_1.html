Nicolas Winding Refn and Oscar-Winning Composer Daniel Blumberg Set for Sounds Like a Movie Festival, Launched by Italy’s CAM Sugar Label

Summary: Italy’s newly launched Sounds Like a Movie (SLAM) festival, backed by CAM Sugar, is set to captivate film and music aficionados with a star-studded lineup that includes director Nicolas Winding Refn, Oscar‑winning composer Daniel Blumberg, and seasoned music supervisor Mary Ramos. Over three days, attendees will experience live performances, curated screenings, and insightful panels that celebrate the artistry of film soundtracks.

Festival Overview

SLAM, held in Rome’s historic Cinecittà studios, is designed as a hybrid event that blends cinema, music, and interactive technology. The festival’s mission is to spotlight the integral role of soundtracks in storytelling, while fostering collaboration between filmmakers, composers, and industry professionals.

Guest Highlights

Director Nicolas Winding Refn, famed for his stylized visuals and sound design in films like Drive, will share his creative process in a live Q&A. Oscar winner Daniel Blumberg, known for his work on The Brutalist, will perform a live score session, showcasing how he transforms cinematic themes into immersive audio experiences. Music supervisor Mary Ramos, whose collaborations with Quentin Tarantino have defined modern soundtrack culture, will lead a panel on curating music for film.

Program Highlights

The three‑day program includes exclusive screenings of Refn’s latest short film, a masterclass on scoring techniques with Blumberg, and a retrospective of iconic film scores curated by Ramos. Live performances by emerging Italian composers will run parallel to the main events, offering a platform for new talent.

Industry Impact

By uniting creative minds from film and music, SLAM aims to spark fresh collaborations and inspire the next generation of soundtrack artists. The partnership with CAM Sugar not only amplifies the festival’s reach but also reinforces Italy’s position as a hub for cinematic and musical innovation.
