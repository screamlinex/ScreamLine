Guillermo del Toro Says He’d ‘Rather Die’ Than Use Generative AI in His Films: ‘Not Interested’

Summary: During a recent sit-down with NPR, Guillermo del Toro declared he would “rather die” than employ generative AI in any of his future films. The legendary director compared the cultural fascination with the technology to the “arrogance” of the madman in his Netflix adaptation of *Frankenstein*, warning that the allure of AI threatens to undermine the craft and soul of filmmaking.

Del Toro’s Stance on AI

In the interview, del Toro made it clear that his creative philosophy is rooted in hands‑on craftsmanship. “I’ve spent my life coaxing stories from the raw material of imagination, not algorithms,” he said. “If we let machines dictate the narrative or visual aesthetic, we lose the human touch that makes cinema resonate.” He cited his own body of work—from the tactile world of *Pan’s Labyrinth* to the meticulous set design of *The Shape of Water*—as evidence of the power of human artistry.

The Broader AI Debate in Hollywood

Del Toro’s comments arrive at a time when Hollywood is grappling with the rise of AI‑generated scripts, deep‑fake actors, and automated visual effects. While some studios tout the cost‑saving potential of AI, others fear it could erode job security for writers, artists, and technicians. The industry’s response has been mixed: a few high‑profile directors have embraced AI tools for brainstorming, while guilds and unions have issued statements urging caution and robust oversight.

Reactions from the Community

Fans of del Toro’s work have largely applauded his commitment to “real” filmmaking, with many social media users echoing his concerns about the commodification of art. Conversely, AI developers and tech evangelists have criticized the director’s stance as “anti‑progress.” A recent tweet from a leading AI research lab noted that the technology could democratize filmmaking for independent creators—an argument that del Toro rebuts by stressing the importance of intentional storytelling.

What This Means for Future Projects

Del Toro’s upcoming projects—rumored to include a new *Hellboy* reboot and a possible collaboration with Guillermo’s wife, Beatriz Treviño—are likely to steer clear of AI‑driven production pipelines. The director’s firm position could influence other filmmakers to adopt more traditional workflows, potentially reshaping the industry's approach to technology. Whether this will spur a renaissance of analog techniques or simply slow the adoption of AI remains to be seen.
