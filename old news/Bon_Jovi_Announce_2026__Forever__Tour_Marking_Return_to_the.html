Bon Jovi Announce 2026 ‘Forever’ Tour Marking Return to the Road After Jon’s Vocal-Cord Surgery

Summary: Bon Jovi has announced the 2026 “Forever” tour, marking the band’s return to the road after lead singer Jon Bon Jovi underwent vocal‑cord surgery. Bon Jovi expressed gratitude to fans and the group for their patience, noting the recovery period allowed him to regain health and prepare for touring.

Tour Details

The “Forever” tour will launch in early 2026, covering North America, Europe, and select Asian markets. The schedule includes 45 dates across 20 cities, with an emphasis on arena venues that can accommodate the band’s high‑energy performances.

Ticket sales are expected to open in September 2025, with a pre‑sale for fan club members followed by a general public release.

Jon Bon Jovi’s Recovery

Jon Bon Jovi underwent vocal‑cord surgery in mid‑2024 after years of strain from touring. He has since completed a structured rehabilitation program and returned to full vocal capability.

In a recent interview, Bon Jovi thanked fans for their support and the band for allowing him the necessary downtime to heal.

Band Lineup

The lineup for the tour will feature founding members Richie Sambora (guitar), Tico Torres (drums), and David Bryan (keyboards), joined by touring bassist Hugh McDonald. New guitarist Phil X will handle solos and rhythm parts.

All members have confirmed their participation and are preparing for the tour’s rigorous schedule.

Fan Reactions

Social media buzz has been largely positive, with fans expressing excitement for the band’s return and appreciation for Bon Jovi’s transparency about his health.

Promotional materials highlight the band’s legacy hits, promising a nostalgic yet fresh concert experience.
