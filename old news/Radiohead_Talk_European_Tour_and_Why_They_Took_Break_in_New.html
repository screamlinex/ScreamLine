Radiohead Talk European Tour and Why They Took Break in New Interview: ‘The Wheels Came Off a Bit’

Summary: In a recent interview, Radiohead discussed the highlights of their European tour and the reasons behind their eight‑year break following the A Moon Shaped Pool tour. Lead vocalist Thom Yorke said the shows felt great, but the band decided to pause before “walking off the cliff.” The band cited creative exhaustion and a need to recalibrate as key factors in their hiatus, and they hinted at a return in the coming years with fresh material.

Tour Highlights

The European leg of the tour was marked by elaborate stage visuals and a setlist that blended new tracks with classic hits. Fans noted the band's tight musicianship and the dynamic interplay between the members.

Hiatus Reasons

Thom Yorke explained that the band felt “the wheels came off a bit” after the extensive touring schedule. Creative fatigue, coupled with a desire to avoid burnout, led them to step back and reassess their artistic direction.

Band Dynamics

During the interview, members highlighted the importance of maintaining a healthy personal and professional balance. They emphasized that the break allowed each member to pursue side projects and recharge creatively.

Future Plans

While no concrete tour dates were announced, the band indicated plans to return with new material and a refreshed lineup of shows. They remain committed to delivering high‑quality performances once they resume touring.
