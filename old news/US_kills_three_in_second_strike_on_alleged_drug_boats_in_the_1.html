Global News / October 23, 2025

US kills three in second strike on alleged drug boats in the Pacific

Summary: The United States Navy carried out its second Pacific strike targeting alleged drug trafficking vessels, killing three crew members. This operation marks the ninth U.S. strike on suspected narcotics smugglers near Latin America, underscoring the growing focus on disrupting maritime drug routes in the region.

Operation Details

The strike, conducted off the coast of Ecuador, involved a U.S. Navy fast attack craft that intercepted a small, low‑profile vessel suspected of carrying cocaine. Using precision-guided munitions, the craft neutralized the vessel and its three crew members, who were believed to be part of a larger trafficking network linked to South American cartels.

Impact on Drug Trafficking

Analysts estimate that each interdiction removes a significant quantity of narcotics from the supply chain—often 10–15 tons of cocaine per vessel. By targeting these vessels, the U.S. aims to disrupt the logistics that allow traffickers to move drugs from production zones in Colombia and Peru to international markets.

Reactions from Latin American Authorities

Officials in Ecuador praised the U.S. intervention as a critical step toward enhancing regional security. The country's Attorney General’s Office confirmed that the vessel had been flagged for suspicious activity and that its interdiction will aid ongoing investigations into transnational drug trafficking.

Future Outlook

Military officials say this is part of a broader campaign—dubbed Operation Pacific Shield—to increase maritime patrols and intelligence sharing with Latin American partners. The U.S. expects to conduct additional strikes and joint exercises in the coming months to maintain pressure on trafficking routes.
