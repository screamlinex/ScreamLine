Watch Bruce Springsteen, Steven Van Zandt Perform ‘Tenth Avenue Freeze-Out’ at the Stone Pony

Summary: Bruce Springsteen and Steven Van Zandt performed “Tenth Avenue Freeze‑Out” at the Stone Pony, followed by “I Don’t Want to Go Home” for a benefit concert supporting Van Zandt’s TeachRock non‑profit.

Event Overview

The iconic New Jersey club hosted a special show featuring Springsteen’s E Street Band and Van Zandt’s backing crew. The concert took place on March 15 at 8:30 p.m., drawing a sold‑out crowd of over 1,200 fans.

Performance Highlights

Springsteen opened with a high‑energy rendition of “Tenth Avenue Freeze‑Out,” showcasing his trademark guitar work and vocal intensity. Van Zandt joined him onstage for a collaborative encore, performing the heartfelt ballad “I Don’t Want to Go Home.” The setlist included several classic hits, all delivered with the duo’s signature chemistry.

TeachRock Benefit

The benefit concert was organized to raise funds for TeachRock, a non‑profit that provides music education to underserved youth. Proceeds from ticket sales, merchandise, and a live auction were earmarked for the organization’s programs in New York and New Jersey.

Audience Reaction

Attendees praised the intimate atmosphere and the musicians’ engagement with the crowd. Social media buzz highlighted the emotional impact of the “I Don’t Want to Go Home” performance, with many fans sharing videos of the moment on Instagram and Twitter.
